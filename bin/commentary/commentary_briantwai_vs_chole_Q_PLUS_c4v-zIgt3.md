## **Red Aggression Crushes Defensive Scaling**
**briantwai (1943) vs chole (1923)** — Standard Set

briantwai dismantles chole's absorb-heavy defense with disciplined red attacker stacking, turning a dead-even early game into a crushing victory by Turn 8.

### Economic Opening: Divergent Philosophies
Both players open textbook (Drone, Drone). On T2, briantwai commits to **Thorium Dynamo**—an unconventional early econ unit that skips Savior entirely. No energy drain, steady gold production. Meanwhile, chole follows traditional **Drone** economy. The AI dislikes both builds, but briantwai's Dynamo choice signals confidence in a fast-attack plan that requires raw gold, not defensive tech.

By T3, chole pivots into **Savior** (briantwai still ignoring defense). This is the moment their strategies crystallize: chole is building walls and **Engineers** for absorb scaling, while briantwai is banking gold for red attackers. Chole's **Blastforge** on T4 is strong—her one moment of eval advantage—but it's the last time she leads. briantwai's subsequent purchases tell the real story: **Mobile Animus** unlocks red production.

### The Pressure Ramp (Turns 5–7)
T5 sees **Tantalum Ray** + **Tarsier** hit the board simultaneously. briantwai is now generating three sources of red damage: his Tarsier is permanent; his Tantalum Ray is a 3-charge powerhouse. chole responds with more Walls and Engineers, but the math is already failing her. Each Wall absorbs one Tarsier hit per turn. Each Engineer adds one absorb. But briantwai's T6 **Tarsier** (a single buy, defying AI) combined with existing units means chole now faces *two* permanent attackers plus the Ray.

T7 doubles down: another **Tantalum Ray** + **Tarsier**. That's three permanent red dealers and a 3-charge unit. chole has four Walls and three Engineers by now—not enough. The eval swings +12 in briantwai's favor. This is the turn the game tips.

---

### Turn 8: The Knockout Punch
**Rhino. Forcefield.** Two units. One decision. Everything changes.

The **Rhino** is a 1/2 unit with prompt: it blocks once, takes 1 damage, and lives to block again next turn. Against briantwai's own Tarsiers, it enables a one-damage-per-turn stamina loop. But here's the disgusting part: briantwai isn't playing the Rhino for himself. He's playing it because it's *fragile* defense that also denies chole's attackers. Meanwhile, the **Forcefield** converts a Drone into an untargetable blocker.

Combined with two **Tantalum Rays**, two **Tarsiers**, and the Rhino itself, briantwai now has a creature-based attack that can slip past absorb entirely (Tantalum Ray ignores Walls via breach-like efficiency; Tarsier is permanent and eats blockers). chole's pure absorb stack—Walls and Engineers—cannot absorb fast enough. The eval jumps **+31 points**. The largest swing of the entire game. chole is done.

T9 seals it: briantwai buys a **Blastforge** and three **Engineers**, pivoting to defense while keeping attackers alive. Chole adds more Walls. T10 is formality—**Wall** and **Forcefield** from briantwai; a single **Wall** from chole.

### What Decided It?
briantwai identified that pure absorb defense (Walls + Engineers) scales worse than concentrated red attackers when the attacker count exceeds the blocker count. By stacking **Tarsier** and **Tantalum Ray** aggressively while ignoring early Savior, he forced chole into a corner where her defensive commitments became sunk costs. The T8 **Rhino + Forcefield** wasn't just a good purchase—it was the moment chole's strategy became mathematically unwinnable. She never pivoted to offense or breachproof tech, and by then, the eval gap was insurmountable.
