## **The Upset: Tempo Over Economy**
### briantwai (1959) vs SpiritFryer (2035) | `B8RFg-RboBM`
**Ranked | 45+45 | 14 turns | briantwai wins**

**Set:** Asteri Cannon, Photonic Fibroid, Drake, Protoplasm, Chrono Filter, Centurion, Arms Race, Barrier, Urban Sentry, Cynestra, Husk

Two visions collide. **SpiritFryer's path:** tech-first economy (dual Conduits, Animus engine, high-impact units like **Centurion** and **Drake**). Build the perfect position, then press. **briantwai's path:** aggressive tempo (early **Chrono Filter** and **Arms Race**) forcing the higher-rated player to react. No margin for error at 1959 rating. Both strategies have merit. Only one survives 14 turns.

The set demands attrition—no pure breachproof units, so **Drake** denial and **Photonic Fibroid** spam become survival mechanisms. **Cynestra** fuels endless throwaway blockers. The player who runs out of resources first loses.

### **The Fork (T1–T5)**

Standard economy opens. Then **T4: briantwai buys Chrono Filter**—the first attacker in a ranked game. Risky. A 67% pickrate unit, but buying a legendary this early signals pure intent: pressure, not grind.

T5 doubles down. **Arms Race.** A scaling engine. briantwai is committing to a long, high-intensity game where unit quality matters more than raw economy.

SpiritFryer responds methodically. **T5: Blastforge + dual Drones.** T6 pivots harder: **Animus + Blastforge.** The tech foundation is being poured—green and red production locked in. SpiritFryer is building the machine. briantwai is building the tempo.

By T6, both have dual Blastforges and dual Conduits. The fork is set: will briantwai's early aggression crack SpiritFryer's econ before it blooms?

### **The Grind (T7–T12)**

T7 answers the question: **both players buy **Centurion**—and more.** briantwai adds **Cynestra**. SpiritFryer adds **Tarsier**. The board is suddenly full of threats.

T8: SpiritFryer adds **Drake**. Absorb denial. briantwai responds T9 with **Centurion + dual Forcefields + Engineers**—defensive saturation.

Here's where the game bifurcates. **T10–T12 is the endgame opening salvo.** Both players stop building economy and start spamming low-cost blockers. T11: briantwai buys 7 units (Walls, Forcefields, Photonic Fibroid, Engineers). T11: SpiritFryer buys 8 units (Rhino, Photonic Fibroid, Blastforge, more blockers).

**T12 is the peak.** SpiritFryer reaches his highest board state: dual **Photonic Fibroids + Engineer + Wall**. This is the moment. But briantwai mirrors the Photonic Fibroids T11 and T13, matching defensive tools turn-for-turn.

Neither player breaks. Both are gridlocked in defence.

### **The Collapse (T13–T14)**

T13: Both players add Photonic Fibroids and Rhinos. The board is *saturated*—every unit slot filled with blockers. Economy is secondary now.

**T14 is the final turn. SpiritFryer buys: Wall, Rhino, Forcefield. Three units.** That's it. At 2035 rating, with the highest board state in the game just two turns prior, SpiritFryer ran dry. Resources exhausted or attack blocked beyond recovery.

briantwai's final turn (T14): **Wall, Forcefield, Forcefield, Engineer.** Four units. Slightly more firepower, but the real story is SpiritFryer's collapse.

**What decided it?** briantwai's early Chrono Filter forced SpiritFryer to defend instead of scale. The legendary became a tempo anchor—not flashy (no game-ending burst), but it demanded responses. SpiritFryer built the superior economy (Animus, dual Blastforges, Centurion, Drake), hit peak eval, and *still couldn't finish.* By T14, the grind had flattened both players' resources—but briantwai had one more unit in hand.

Tempo beats economy when neither player can execute a knockout. The lower-rated player held the line and won attrition.
