## xYotsu vs Hey — `uP8mG-tr75d`
**Ranked Arena | 60+60/60 | 40 turns | xYotsu (P1) wins**
**xYotsu 2232 | Hey 2043**

**Set:** Shadowfang, Doomed Mech, Vivid Drone, Innervi Field, Drake, Apollo, Mega Drone, Galvani Drone

This set is all about **blue**. Drake (12BB, 2 atk/turn + sac Blastforge for 2 more), Apollo (13BBB, snipe anything ≤3HP), and Doomed Mech (9BB, prompt 5HP blocker + 2 atk) all demand massive Blastforge infrastructure. Mega Drone converts surplus attack into 4g/turn of economy — the late-game efficiency tool. Shadowfang (red) and Vivid Drone go completely untouched.

### The Opening (T1-10): Greedy Mirror

Both players read the set identically. Pure economy for 8 turns — Drones, Engineers, then a race to Blastforges. By T9-10 both have **triple Blastforge**, and Hey pushes to a fourth on T10 alongside the first Galvani Drone. Zero early aggression. On a set where Apollo snipes low-HP units, cheap attackers are just snipe fodder — better to greed hard and get to the big tech first.

### The Commit (T11-16): Apollo Mirror into Drake Arms Race

**T11 is the hinge.** xYotsu drops **Apollo** — snipe anything ≤3HP. On this board that means Galvani Drones, Engineers, and Innervi Fields are all targets. Hey answers T12 with their own Apollo + Gauss Cannon.

Then both escalate fast: xYotsu gets Drake T13, Hey matches T14 with Drake + Innervi Field + Forcefield. By T15-16 it's fully committed — xYotsu picks up a second Drake, Hey responds with **two Drakes in one turn** plus double Doomed Mech. Both players now have Apollo + 2 Drakes and a wall of Doomed Mechs. The arms race is locked in.

T17 brings the last key tech: both players pick up **Mega Drone** (xYotsu T17, Hey T20), converting excess attack into 4g/turn of sustained economy. With one each, the economy conversion is even — neither player gets an edge here.

== MESSAGE 3 ==

### The Grind (T21-35): Doomed Mech Rotation

This is where the game becomes a war of attrition. Both players settle into a rhythm: Doomed Mech (prompt 5HP + 2 atk, lifespan 5 — needs constant replacement), Steelsplitter/Gauss Cannon (permanent attack), Innervi Field (cheap prompt green defense). Repeat for 15 turns.

**The clock diverges.** xYotsu is under crushing time pressure — 0.0s bank on turns 21, 23, 25, 27, 29, 33, 39. Turn 27 is brutal: **90 clicks, 60.2 seconds, bank at 0.2s**. Meanwhile Hey stays relatively comfortable at 5-20s bank.

But the purchasing patterns diverge too. xYotsu leans into persistent attack — accumulating Steelsplitters and Gauss Cannons turn after turn. Hey leans defensive — more Walls, more Innervi Fields, more Forcefields. In a war of attrition, the player who maintains offensive output while defending efficiently is the one applying pressure. The one forced to over-invest in defense is slowly falling behind.

== MESSAGE 4 ==

### The Kill (T36-40): Position Collapse

T36: Hey plays the third Drake — a desperation move. Drake sacs a Blastforge for burst attack, but that Blastforge isn't coming back. Short-term power at the cost of long-term blue production.

T37-38 tell the story. xYotsu buys 7 units (Wall, Doomed Mech, Conduit, 2 Engineers, Innervi Field, Forcefield). Hey manages just **3 units** in 65 clicks — Innervi Field, Wall, Forcefield. The economy is gone. All those Blastforges sacrificed to Drake abilities, all those resources spent on Walls and Forcefields — there's nothing left to buy attackers with.

T40: Hey's final turn. Wall, Innervi Field. Two purchases. That's the sound of a position crumbling.

### What Decided This Game?

Not one single tech choice — both players built virtually identical armies (Apollo, 2+ Drakes, Mega Drone, Doomed Mech rotation). The ~200 rating point gap showed up in **efficiency through the grind**. xYotsu consistently bought more persistent attackers while Hey over-invested in defense. Over 15+ turns of attrition, that imbalance compounded — Hey needed more defense because xYotsu had more attack, which left less for Hey's own attack, which meant less pressure on xYotsu, which meant xYotsu could invest even more in attack.

Hey's third Drake (T36) was the final symptom, not the cause. By that point, the squeeze was already fatal.

40 turns of Doomed Mech rotation, an Apollo mirror, and a 200-rating-point gap that expressed itself one Steelsplitter at a time. Slow strangulation.
