# The Tempo Tax | FxCfR-K49T+
**Surfinite (1856) vs Kolento (2222)** — Result: Surfinite wins in 15 turns

**Set:** Animus, Vai Mauronax, Plasmafier, Ebb Turbine, Forcefield

An upset between a 1856 and a 2222. The random set offers splashy options — Plasmafier, Vai Mauronax, Ebb Turbine — but neither player buys any of them. This game is won and lost in the base set. The only random unit purchased all game is a single Forcefield.

Both players open identically: T1 Drone, T2 Drone + Thorium Dynamo. Then both save gold on T3, buying nothing. The fork comes on T4: Surfinite picks up a Blastforge while Kolento buys another Drone. That one-turn tempo advantage on blue tech defines the rest of the game.

== MESSAGE 2 ==
The eval tells the story immediately. After Surfinite's T4 Blastforge, the eval jumps to 66% in his favour. Kolento doesn't get his own Blastforge until T5, paired with a Drone. By the time Kolento has blue infrastructure, Surfinite is already a full turn ahead in Wall production — T5 Wall while Kolento is still setting up.

From T6-T7, both players buy Drones, Engineers, and a Galvani Drone. Nothing flashy — just economic build-up. Kolento's eval sits at 31% and won't climb back.

== MESSAGE 3 ==
Surfinite's T8 is the power spike. He drops a massive 7-unit buy: Conduit, three Engineers, two Galvani Drones, and a Wall. That's the full gold reserve converted into infrastructure and defense. He follows up on T10 with Mahar Rectifier + Engineer + Wall — rotating pseudo-absorb plus more blocking.

Kolento tries to answer. T9 he picks up Tesla Coil and a Forcefield (the only random-set unit in the entire game). T11 he buys a Conduit, three Engineers, a Drone, and a Wall. But the eval keeps sliding — 27% at T11, 21% soon after.

== MESSAGE 4 ==
The final turns are Kolento scrambling. T13 he buys a Steelsplitter — his only attacker all game — plus three Engineers. T14 is Engineer + Wall. But the deficit is too deep. The eval reads 36% and falling.

Surfinite closes it at 73% with a final Wall on T15. The fortress holds.

**Why it worked:** Surfinite's T4 Blastforge gave him a one-turn lead on blue tech. In a base-set grind where both players are buying the same units, one turn of tempo compounds — earlier Walls mean more absorb, which means more Drones survive, which means more gold for the next turn. Kolento never found an angle to break the symmetry. `FxCfR-K49T+`
