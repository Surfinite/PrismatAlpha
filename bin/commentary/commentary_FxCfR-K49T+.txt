# Giant Killer | FxCfR-K49T+
**Surfinite (1856) vs Kolento (2222)** — Result: Surfinite wins in 15 rounds

**Random Set:** Mahar Rectifier, Colossus, Tesla Coil, Militia, Galvani Drone, Feral Warden, Thorium Dynamo, Vai Mauronax, Plasmafier, Ebb Turbine

A 366-point upset. This is a rich 10-unit set with big flashy options — Vai Mauronax, Colossus, Ebb Turbine — but neither player touches any of them. Instead both players lean into Galvani Drone economy, Thorium Dynamo green generation, and Tesla Coil chill. The game is decided by tempo in blue tech and Wall production.

**Opening (T1-T4)**

Both players open T1 with double Drone. T2 diverges: Surfinite takes Drone + Thorium Dynamo, while Kolento goes double Drone — prioritising raw gold over green. T3 Surfinite doubles down on the green economy with Galvani Drone + second Thorium Dynamo. Kolento buys a single Drone.

The critical fork is T4. Surfinite buys Blastforge + Drone. Kolento buys... just a Drone. That's the moment. Surfinite is a full turn ahead on blue tech, which means a full turn earlier on Walls, which means absorb comes online sooner. At 1856 vs 2222 you'd expect Kolento to be the one seizing tempo — but Surfinite plays the sharper game here.

**Development (T5-T7)**

T5 Surfinite converts the Blastforge into an immediate Wall — prompt 3HP absorber soaking 2 damage per turn. Kolento's T5 is enormous: double Animus, double Blastforge, double Drone. That's a massive tech commitment — 2 red per turn from each Animus means 4R available, but he needs to actually spend it all every turn or it's wasted. Going double Animus is a gamble that the Tarsier pipeline will stay efficient.

T6 Surfinite picks up Plasmafier + Drone. Plasmafier converts green into red, giving flexible access to Tarsiers without committing to an Animus. Kolento gets his first attacker: one Tarsier plus a Galvani Drone.

T7: Surfinite buys Tesla Coil + Engineer + Drone. The Tesla Coil gives 1 chill per turn — freezing an enemy unit to prevent blocking. Kolento answers with double Rhino + Engineer, getting prompt blockers and click-attackers online.

**Midgame Escalation (T8-T10)**

T8 is Surfinite's power turn: Conduit, three Engineers, three Galvani Drones, and a Wall. Eight units in one turn — that's a full economic conversion into granularity (lots of small blockers) plus Wall absorb. Kolento's T8 is more modest: Engineer, Galvani Drone, Tarsier. He's building attack but the pipeline feels thin.

T9 Surfinite buys a second Tesla Coil, two Forcefields, and two Drones. Double Tesla Coil means 2 chill per turn — that's devastating against Kolento's Tarsiers (1 HP each, frozen by a single chill point). Kolento responds with three Engineers, three Galvani Drones, and another Tarsier — still trying to scale.

T10 Surfinite gets Mahar Rectifier + Engineer + Wall. The Mahar Rectifier provides 1 reusable absorb — pseudo-Wall that regenerates. Between Walls, Mahar Rectifier, and Engineers, Surfinite's defense is becoming a fortress. Kolento buys Blastforge + Engineer, finally teching into blue for his own Walls.

**The Grind (T11-T15)**

From here it's attrition. Surfinite buys Wall every single turn from T11-T15, plus steady Drones and Engineers. T12 he adds three Forcefields — emergency defense suggesting Kolento's attack is starting to bite. T15 he finally picks up a Gauss Cannon — his first dedicated attacker, fragile but breachproof at 4 HP.

Kolento gets his first Wall at T11 (six turns after Surfinite's first). He buys Rhinos at T11 and T14 for dual-purpose block/attack, plus Walls and Engineers. But he's always one step behind on absorb.

The chill advantage is the silent killer. Surfinite's two Tesla Coils freeze 2 of Kolento's units every turn. Against a Tarsier-heavy army where each attacker has just 1 HP, that's 2 damage completely negated. Over 8+ turns that compounds into 16+ effective HP saved.

**Verdict**

Surfinite wins an impressive upset against a player 366 rating points higher. Three decisions made it:

1. **T4 Blastforge** — one turn ahead on blue tech cascaded into earlier Walls and sustained absorb advantage
2. **Double Tesla Coil (T7, T9)** — chill hard-counters Kolento's Tarsier-heavy attack, neutralising 2 damage/turn
3. **Plasmafier over Animus** — Surfinite's green-to-red conversion was more efficient than Kolento's double Animus commitment, which demanded perfect red spending every turn

Kolento's double Animus on T5 was ambitious but risky. When your opponent has chill to freeze your Tarsiers, the red production goes to waste. The rating gap said Kolento should win, but Prismata doesn't care about ratings — it cares about tempo. `FxCfR-K49T+`
