## xYotsu vs Hey — `uP8mG-tr75d`
**Ranked Arena | 60+60/60 | 39 turns | xYotsu (P1) wins**

**Set:** Shadowfang, Doomed Mech, Vivid Drone, Innervi Field, Drake, Apollo, Mega Drone, Galvani Drone

This set screams **blue**. Drake (12BB, passive 2 atk + sac Blastforge for 2 more), Apollo (13BBB, snipe anything ≤3HP), and Doomed Mech (9BB, prompt 5HP blocker + 2 atk) all demand massive Blastforge infrastructure. The question isn't *whether* you go blue — it's how greedily you can ramp into it.

### The Opening (T1-10): Drone City

Both players read the set identically and go for an extremely greedy econ ramp. The first 8 turns are pure economy — Drones, Engineers, Blastforges. By T9-10, both players have committed to **triple Blastforge**. Hey actually goes to *four* on T10. Clear signal: both are racing to Apollo.

Notably absent is any early aggression. No Tarsiers, no Steelsplitters, no Rhinos. On a set where Apollo snipes low-HP units and the endgame is Doomed Mech rotation, early chip damage is just throwing resources away.

### The Commit (T11-14): Apollo Mirror

**Turn 11 is the hinge.** xYotsu drops **Apollo**. Its snipe (destroy any unit ≤3HP) is devastating here — picks off Galvani Drones (1HP, undefendable), Engineers (1HP), and crucially **Innervi Fields** (3HP). It demands an immediate response.

Hey answers T12 with their own Apollo + Gauss Cannon. The mirror is locked in.

Then the arms race: xYotsu gets Drake + Steelsplitter + Wall (T13), Hey responds with Drake + Wall + Innervi Field + Forcefield (T14). Hey's early Innervi Field is forward-thinking — cheap green defense that regenerates, buying time for the blue army to come online.

### The Escalation (T15-20): Drake Stacking and Mega Drone Innovation

The middle game explodes. xYotsu gets a **second Drake** on T15. Hey fires back T16 with the biggest buy of the game: **Drake + double Doomed Mech + Blastforge + Innervi Field + 3 Drones + Engineer** — nine purchases in one turn.

Then T17 — xYotsu unveils the tech that arguably wins this game: **Mega Drone**. At a cost of 5g + 3H + 4 attack, Mega Drone converts offensive pressure into 4 gold/turn of sustained economy. xYotsu eventually buys **three** of these vs Hey's one. That's 12g/turn vs 4g/turn of converted economy — a structural advantage that compounds every single turn.

== MESSAGE 3 ==

### The Grind (T21-35): Attrition Warfare

Both players settle into a rhythm: Doomed Mech (prompt defense + 2 atk), Steelsplitter/Gauss Cannon (permanent attack), Innervi Field (cheap prompt defense), top up with Galvani Drones. Repeat. Every. Single. Turn.

xYotsu buys **11 Doomed Mechs** total, Hey buys **10**. With lifespan 5, these expire constantly and must be replaced. The player who sustains the rotation longer wins.

**But the clock tells a different story.** xYotsu is under crushing time pressure — multiple turns at exactly 0.0s bank. Turn 27 is a war crime against the clock: **90 clicks, 12 undos, 60.2s spent**. Meanwhile Hey plays comfortably with 5-20s of bank.

And yet xYotsu is *winning*. The Mega Drone economy edge means slightly more buys every turn. Steelsplitters diverge: xYotsu accumulates **7** to Hey's **2**, backed by 7 Gauss Cannons to Hey's 6. More persistent attackers = more pressure = Hey spends more on defense = less for counterattack. The slow squeeze is on.

== MESSAGE 4 ==

### The Kill (T36-39): The Position Collapses

T36: Hey makes a desperate play — **third Drake** + Wall + double Innervi Field + Forcefield. Drake sacs a Blastforge for burst attack. One big push.

T37: xYotsu absorbs it with heavy defense — Wall, Doomed Mech, Conduit, double Engineer, Innervi Field, Forcefield. Seven purchases. The punch lands on a brick wall.

T38 reveals Hey's position is crumbling: just **Innervi Field + Wall + Forcefield** bought despite 65 clicks of frantic unit management. The Drakes have eaten the Blastforges, the gold goes to replacement blockers, and there's nothing left for counterattack.

T39: xYotsu's final buy — Doomed Mech, Innervi Field, Gauss Cannon, Drone — and that's enough. Hey's defenses can't hold.

### What Decided This Game?

**Mega Drone. Three of them.** Near-identical openings, identical Apollo timings, similar Drake investment — the difference was sustained economy. xYotsu's 3 Mega Drones produced 12 extra gold/turn vs Hey's 4g/turn. Over a 20-turn grind, that's ~160 gold of extra purchasing power.

Hey's better time management wasn't enough. Sometimes in Prismata, the player scrambling at 0.0s with 12 undos per turn is the one winning on the board.

*What if Hey had matched Mega Drone production earlier?* Hey's second Mega Drone didn't come until T20, when xYotsu already had 2. If Hey had prioritized conversion on T18-19, the grind might have played out very differently.

39 turns. 11 Doomed Mechs each. An Apollo mirror. Decided by who converted their economy better. A proper slugfest.
