**1. One-line verdict**  
The architecture is exceptionally well-thought-out and the two-stage pipeline perfectly fits the model constraints, but the prompt caching strategy and programmatic verification have critical flaws that will cause brittle execution and inflated costs.

**2. What's good**  
* **Two-stage pipeline:** Separating analysis from narrative is exactly the right call. It acts as a structural Chain-of-Thought, preventing Haiku from writing dry listicles or missing strategic turning points while trying to juggle data extraction and prose formatting simultaneously.
* **Structured Data foundation:** Relying on the C++ eval and resource-validated buys instead of raw log feeds is a massive advantage that will drastically reduce hallucinations. 
* **Cost optimization:** Defaulting to Haiku
