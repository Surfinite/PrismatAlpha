# Category 5: Openings & Build Orders

## Build Order Notation
> Source: Common Openings (wiki)

Standard notation for base set units in build orders:
- **A**: Animus
- **B**: Blastforge
- **C**: Conduit
- **D**: Drone
- **E**: Engineer
- **F**: Forcefield
- **G**: Gauss Cannon
- **R**: Rhino
- **S**: Steelsplitter
- **T**: Tarsier
- **W**: Wall

Each line = one turn. "/" separates P1/P2 turns. "..." before a line means it's P2's turn.

## General Tech Progressions
> Source: Common Openings (wiki) + Standard Style

### Natural Conduit (P2)
Player 2 has the first opportunity to build a Conduit without cutting Drones. Particularly advantageous in sets with strong green units.
```
1. ...DD
2. ...DDC
```

### Turn 3 Blastforge/Animus (P1)
Player 1's first opportunity for blue/red tech without cutting Drones.
```
1. DD
2. DD
3. DDB
```
or
```
1. DD
2. DD
3. DDA
```

### Big Blue (P1)
Large economy while wasting few resources. Ideal for defensive sets or strong blue units like Drake.
```
1. DD
2. DDE
3. DDD
4. DDDB
```

### 3rd Engineer Blastforge (P1)

```
4. DDDB
```

### Elyot Animus (P1)

## P1 vs P2 Asymmetry
> Source: Common Openings (wiki) + Standard Style

"The majority of these openings are only available to Player 2, due to starting with an extra drone. In most cases, Player 1 will be one or two gold short when attempting a P2 opening."

"However, many of the openings do become available to Player 1 if Doomed Drone or, in some cases Wild Drone, is in the set."

Examples: Delayed Tia Thurnax, Tatsu Nullifier Rush, and Double Scorchilla become P1-viable with Doomed Drone.

"If the biggest absorber has 5 or more health, go for a third Engineer build."

## Tech Setup Heuristics
> Source: Standard Style (307th)

After deciding economy size, plan tech to reach your absorber and attackers:

"If the biggest absorber is an extremely tech-heavy unit like Centurion, this will account for pretty much all of your tech — you need at least one Conduit, two Blastforges, and one Animus to make a Centurion. Using the guideline above, this will require 21 Gold per turn to spend."

"If the biggest absorber is very cheap on tech — say, Doomed Wall — then you'll have extra gold to spend on attack tech buildings."

## Opening Theory Limitations
> Source: Common Openings (wiki)

"Unlike chess and some other games of complete information, opening book study is largely ineffective in Prismata due to the random unit set present in each game. However, it can still be helpful to be aware of some common openings."

"Many aggressive builds are difficult or impossible to overcome if the opponent does not anticipate them and adjust accordingly. Familiarizing oneself with these builds is helpful in finding opportunities to use these tactics and, more importantly, learning to counter them."

### Overteching Warning
> Source: Yujiri — Basics

"Another common new player mistake is to buy a lot of tech buildings without enough Drones to make use of them. If you buy two Blastforges, two Animuses, and two Conduits with just 15 Drones, you won't have nearly enough gold to spend all of your tech."

307th's rule of thumb confirmed by Yujiri: "One Conduit requires 3 Drones, one Blastforge requires 5, and one Animus 8."

## Secret Opening Book (Expert Builds)
> Source: User-provided research (Prismata community, high-level play)

### Shadowfang Counter Build
When opponent telegraphs Shadowfang rush (double Animus setup): respond with fast Wall + Engineer defense. Shadowfangs' 1 HP makes them breach-vulnerable — if you survive the initial pressure, your absorb advantage wins.

### Double Scorchilla Setup
P2 aggressive: DD/DDC/DA/Scorchilla×2. Requires red + green tech. Strong burst on alternate turns, but "falls apart against Wall" — only use when Wall supply is contested.

### Flame Animus + Shadowfang Line
DD/DB/D(Flame Animus)/DD(Shadowfang). Aggressive red build that gets Shadowfang out fast through Flame Animus tech. High risk, high reward.

### Electrovore Pump
Early Animus into Electrovore spam. "First two seem like 4R Fast Tarsiers" because starting Engineers provide free energy. Also gives immediate "potshots" that can kill Engineers before Wall arrives.

### Feral Warden + Grimbotch Skip
In sets with both Feral Warden and Grimbotch: skip Grimbotch entirely. Feral Warden provides both attack AND absorb, making Grimbotch's short-term burst redundant.

## Opening Transpositions
> Source: User-provided research (Prismata community)

### Trinity Drone Transpositions
The P2 Trinity Drone opening (DD/DDC/D+Trinity) transposes into multiple tech paths:
- After D+Trinity, 6 gold available → Animus (aggressive), Blastforge (defensive), or C+E (expand)
- With Trinity clicked: same efficiency as 3rd Conduit opening
- With Trinity unclicked: keeps green banked, delays commitment

These transpositions make Trinity Drone one of the most flexible opening units because the decision point comes AFTER purchase, not before.
