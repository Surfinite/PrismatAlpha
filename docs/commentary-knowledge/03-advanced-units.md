## Unit Efficiency Analysis (Mathematical)
> Source: Yujiri (former #2 ranked player) — "Intro to the Math Behind Prismata" + "Math Data" (yujiri.xyz, archived)

### Inflation Theory
"Inflation" = how much more valuable something is now vs next turn. With Drones: 9 gold now → 12 gold next turn (3 Drones + 3 gold produced) = **1.33x inflation**. Higher inflation means faster units (prompt, short buildtime) are relatively better.

**Resource values** (derived from tech building balance):
- Gold = 1.00, Green = 1.33, Blue = 1.67, Red = 1.00, Energy ≈ 0.56

**Barrier Value (BV)**: How much gold 1 point of defense costs. Base set BV ≈ 2.3 (Wall-based).

### Immolite vs Tarsier Decision Tree
> Source: Yujiri — "When to Buy Immolite versus Tarsier"

1. If Tarsier's buildtime can deny absorb → **buy Tarsier**
2. If Immolite's finesse exploits the enemy → **buy Immolite**
3. If Immolite lets you spend tech you'd otherwise waste → **buy Immolite**
4. If Immolite doesn't force prompt defense → **buy Tarsier**
5. If Immolite forces efficient defense (Aegis, Polywall) → **probably Tarsier**
6. If Immolite forces medium defense (Wall) → **probably Immolite**
7. If Immolite forces inefficient defense (Forcefield or worse) → **buy Immolite**

"Super greedy attackers with a unique feel":
- **Tatsu Nullifier** — "Hard-counters almost every big defender: Energy Matrix, Doomed Wall, Defense Grid, Centurion, Plexo Cell"

## Absorber Rankings
> Source: 307th — "Absorber Rankings" (prismatalibrary.blog, Feb 2018)

Ranked on three criteria: **value** (damage nullification efficiency), **convenience** (tech/build requirements), **clunkiness** (difficulty obtaining full value).

### S Tier
- **Centurion** — Highest value absorber. Prompt and reliable. Rarely skipped unless competing against powerful Big Red units. Tech: Green-Blue-Red.

### A Tier
- **Defense Grid** — Second-strongest but requires three Blue tech. Vulnerable to Chill. Benefits from Blue sinks.
- **Energy Matrix** — "Really good value, convenient, and reliable." 4 absorb/turn at 8BB cost.
- **Colossus** — Third-strongest by value but extremely clunky. Fragile with 4 supply limit; requires 7 damage/turn for full absorption.
- **Xeno Guardian** — Vigilant: attacks while defending. Good value with reliability.

### B Tier
- **Infusion Grid** — Convenient blue absorber, superior to Wall. Bridge to larger absorbers.
- **Bombarder** — Strong overall; best absorption at 1 stamina when attacking provides no pressure.
- **Urban Sentry** — Vigilant Blue-Green. Excellent first attacker despite slightly weaker value than Infusion Grid.
- **Omega Splitter** — Better value than Infusion Grid but prohibitively expensive (15BBB).
- **Doomed Wall** — Similar to Infusion Grid. 3-turn lifespan creates Chill vulnerability.
- **Xaetron** — Fragile but gains 4 health/turn regardless of damage. Excels against Chill through threat manipulation.
- **Doomed Mech** — Comparable value to Xaetron. Lifespan creates Chill vulnerability.
- **Mahar Rectifier** — Permanent fragile unit requiring two copies for reliable rotation.
- **Feral Warden** — Red-Green absorber. Stronger early, loses value as games progress.
- **Odin** — Primarily attacker; worth buying before opponent threatens damage due to 4 threat value.
- **Chieftain** — Primarily soak unit; works as absorber by timing lifespan correctly.

### C Tier
- **Protoplasm** — Weak early, strong late-game. Opposite of Feral Warden.
- **Wall** — Convenient but weak. Default when no better absorber exists.

### Rhino Tier
- **Rhino** — Pure Red absorber. Weakest option due to Red's aggressive nature.

### Special Category
- **Arka Sodara** — Insanely high value under right circumstances, but costs 7 attack. Creates paradox where both players avoid buying it first, giving decisive advantage to second Arka buyer. Does NOT affect economy sizing like standard absorbers.

**Commentary insight**: Economy size correlates with absorber strength — better absorbers enable larger economies. Absorbers providing attack (Centurion, Xeno Guardian, Odin) reward aggressive play alongside defense.

## Noticeable Units (Expert Analysis)
> Source: Foxclear — Advanced Strategy Guide: Noticeable Units (wiki)

Units that cannot be ignored and will shape any set they appear in.

### Centurion — The Strongest Unit
"Currently, the strongest unit in the game, and it is really rare that I will not go for it." Even worth buying when opponent can freeze it (like against Vai Mauronax), because of the high defense tempo and aggression it provides. Occasionally, ultra-aggressive sets won't let you buy it, but "most often, Centurion tames even the angry Red stuff."

### Tia Thurnax — The Highest Impact
"The unit with the highest immediate impact." A "naked Tia" (without other attackers) rarely succeeds, but it must be bought during the game. **Timing is critical**: higher economy = buy later. "In a fast, aggressive game with Wall as best absorber, a quick Tia along with 2 Tarsiers can often get the job done."

**Counter early Tia with blue defense only**: "If your opponent does super early Tia, survive by defending with blue. Build Blastforge, Walls, Engineers, wait for them to pass out." — amalloy, "WDIL: ErikTheRed vs Scrazzor" (YouTube). The mobile Animus rush into Tia can be weathered purely through blue defense without matching their aggression.

### Thunderhead — The Anti-Aggression
"Provided there is no burst damage available (Gaussite Symbiote, Cluster Bolt), Thunderhead will make any aggressive strategy crumble." Even staying 2 turns is quite valuable. Before buying, evaluate the maximum damage your opponent can get to shut it down: "if it is equal to 11 or more, think again!" **Amazing synergy with Redeemer**: buying Redeemer right after Thunderhead creates massive pressure; opponent's Gauss Charges crash into Thunderhead on its last lifespan turn.

### Xaetron — The Anti-Abuse
Best unit against any kind of defensive abuse — it regenerates health regardless of damage patterns. Key tips:
- Buy it against 0-attack strategies (synched Iso Kronus or Scorchillas)
- Never click unless opponent isn't attacking it (reaching 12 health) or you desperately need pressure
- **Dual-absorber strategy**: Absorb on Xaetron one turn (when high health), overdefend Xaetron next turn to absorb on backup absorber. With Wall: averages 5 absorb (vs 4 on Xaetron alone). With stronger absorbers: even better.

### The Wincer — The Biggest Cannon
Highest damage output from a single unit. "The pressure can usually not be defended in one turn, and the first shot rarely gets there. But the second shot is usually the strong one." **Key insight**: you can HOLD The Wincer — the pressure alone is often enough. "If it is defended with frontline units (Polywall, Hannibull), try to set up exact damage with permanent attackers and hold The Wincer while taking down the frontline." Against 1-lifespan defense (Plexo Cell), don't shoot — let the defense expire in vain.

### Arka Sodara — The Largest Value
"Whenever Arka Sodara is in the set, you practically have to get at some point the 7 attack threshold with BBR tech." Counter-Arka (buying your own Arka with exactly 7 damage to deny the 6 absorb) is well-known but "do not overestimate the counter-Arka — getting Arka first means you get to click it first." The concept loses its point as damage exceeds 7. **Trap**: Be careful with R sinks like Perforator or Tyranno Smorcus — attacking with them spends the Red needed to buy Arka.

### Thermite Core — The Most Annoying
"Recently buffed, this unit is difficult to deal with. With the right set-up, you can deny absorb completely multiple turns in a row for a ridiculous cost." **Against it**: defend for exact every turn without using high-value absorbers while Thermites have full value, or go breachproof. **Playing it**: aim for even attack values matching 2× your Thermite Core count. Odd values reduce Thermite value (building only 1 Pixie instead of 2).

## Unit-Specific Tactical Patterns
> Source: User-provided research (Prismata blog, community analysis)

### Odin — Full Build Notation & Timing
**Standard Odin build (P1)**: DD/DDE/DDD/DDDB/DDD/BBB+Odin (costs 20G+3B, comes with 3 Steelsplitters, click to sacrifice a Splitter for 4 attack).

**P2 Turn 8 Gold Storage**: P2 can buy Odin by storing gold on Turn 7 (floating ~4 gold) to have exactly 20G+3B on Turn 8. Creates an earlier Odin than standard builds.

**Three equilibrium exceptions** where Odin isn't worth buying despite appearing dominant:
1. **Cynestra + Pixie** — Cynestra assassinates Odin's Steelsplitters, denying click value
2. **Wincer cliff** — Wincer's burst can overwhelm Odin's defense before it generates enough value
3. **Old Drake** (pre-nerf) — Drake's Blastforge sacrifice produces enough threat to outpace Odin

**Nerf history**: Odin nerfed from 5HP to 3HP. "Odin is the most player-1-favouring unit in high-level Prismata play." The nerf made it more vulnerable to breach targeting.

### Protoplasm — Universally Strong
"Protoplasm is always good if no one has blue answers." Without blue tech (no Wall, no Forcefield), Protoplasm's growth is unchecked. The community considers it universally strong in sets lacking blue-based defense.

### Scorchilla Rush — Over-Selected by P2
P2 Double Scorchilla rush is over-selected because it "looks scary but falls apart against Wall." Efficient defense (Wall absorbs the burst, Engineers handle off-turns) nullifies the pressure. Only strong when Wall supply is contested or opponent lacks blue.

### Trinity Drone — Breach-Tolerant Glue
Beyond breachproof economy, Trinity Drone acts as "breach-tolerant glue" — it continues producing green after breach (unlike Conduit, which isn't targeted but whose green goes to waste without economy). Enables spare green conversion to Forcefields during emergencies.

### Kinetic Driver — Defense Reducer via Tech-Sniping
Kinetic Driver's unique value is as a "defense reducer" — it snipes tech buildings (Blastforges, Animuses) to deny future defensive purchases. The timing window is critical: snipe early enough to prevent absorber setup, late enough to have gathered sufficient attack. "The best Kinetic Driver targets are tech buildings the opponent is counting on for their defensive plan."

### Nivo Charge — Burst Pattern
Nivo Charge enables a "stored green" burst pattern: accumulate green over 2-3 turns, then buy 2-3 Nivos simultaneously for massive freeze pressure. The burst forces either a breach or a massively inefficient defense response. However, "if your opponent can defend it, his defense will stick around for the following turns, so you have to rebuy the same amount of Nivos" — and supply is scarce.

### Ebb Turbine — ~2 Turn Payback
Ebb Turbine pays back its gold cost in approximately 2 turns, making it one of the fastest-returning economic investments. However, its value is heavily set-dependent — it needs good blue sinks to justify the slot.

### Wincer Rush Shell
Standard Wincer build: DD/DDC/AB/D1 forces P1 into DDE response. The Wincer threat alone shapes the entire game: "even unclicked, The Wincer is doing work because your opponent must prepare defense for the potential shot."

### Iceblade Golem — Draw Potential
Iceblade Golem games have a 0.98% draw rate — the highest of any unit. This is because Iceblade's high HP (7) and freeze ability create stalemate-prone endgames where neither player can break through.

### Plexo Cell — Confirmed Fragile
Plexo Cell has the Fragile keyword, meaning damage to it is permanent. This is sometimes overlooked but is crucial for evaluation — it means Plexo Cell cannot absorb, only soak.

## Balance Change History
> Source: Prismata Blog — Balance Patches + Developer analysis

| Unit | Change | Date | Rationale |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| Odin | 5HP → 3HP | Jan 2019 | "Most P1-favouring unit." Experiments showed lower HP still playable. |
| Wild Drone | Redesigned entirely | Jan 2019 | "An entirely new unit with completely different synergies." |
| Militia | Buffed (dynamics) | Jan 2019 | "Top player Msven championed this as a way of making the unit more dynamic." |
| Redeemer | No change (confirmed) | Jan 2019 | "Some players suggested P2 bias, but statistics showed little to no bias." |
| Vendium Scrap | Design exploration | 2018 | Designed to create more openings; Vendium Scrap lets P1 access P2-exclusive builds. |

**Design philosophy**: "Rather than simply hitting obvious outliers with nerfs, we took a more nuanced approach — adjusting units that created undesirable patterns while also buffing units that were underused."

**Combat removal philosophy**: The developers intentionally avoided unit-on-unit direct combat. All damage goes through the pooled attack/defense system. This prevents "rock-paper-scissors at the unit level" and ensures strategic depth comes from army composition and timing, not individual unit matchups.

## Unit Insights from Yujiri's Set Analyses (2-9)
> Source: Yujiri — Set analyses 2-9 (archived from yujiri.xyz/prismata/, Wayback Machine Dec 2022)

### Doomed Wall Counter — Shiver Yeti
Shiver Yeti hard-counters Doomed Wall: freeze on 1 lifespan means zero absorb. Must set up 2 Shiver Yetis *before* all resources go to defense, or opponent safely uses Doomed Walls.

### Drake vs Ossified Drone
Drake's threat loses value against Ossified Drone — vigilant defense blocks while still producing gold. Clicking Drake to kill the Ossified Drone means replacing Blastforge, which wastes red when you're already on Animus.

### Shadowfang + Perforator Combo
Perforator solves Shadowfang's two main problems: (1) wasted 4th red from 2 Animuses, (2) red-spending difficulty in late-game defense. Perforator as red sink makes Shadowfang significantly more viable.

### Cryo Ray + Iso Kronus Sync
Cryo Ray solves the synchronization penalty of Iso Kronus: with 2 Conduits, buy 2 Iso Kronuses when they fire and 2 Cryo Rays when they don't. Full sync power without efficiency loss.

### Arka Sodara Timing
"Buy Arka before Defense Grid" — if you buy Grid first, Arka's promptness is wasted. Buy Arka when opponent has ~10 attack, then get Grid immediately after.

### Vivid Drone as Deadeye Counter
Deadeye Operative targets units named "Drone" — Vivid Drone is not named "Drone." Converting economy to Vivids completely neutralizes Deadeye strategies.

### Fission Turret + Cluster Bolt
Clicking dying Fission Turrets generates 3 green each — spend on Cluster Bolts for massive burst. More efficient than Hellhound when all green is spent. "Talk about a combo."

### Omega Splitter Weakness to Freeze
Nivo Charge freezing Omega Splitter denies its absorb, and Omega's clunky/non-prompt nature means you can't defend the accompanying Cluster Bolt burst either.

### Trinity Drone + Asteri Cannon Breachproof
Powerful breachproof combo: Trinity Drone converts economy, Asteri Cannon provides breachproof attack. Counters both Valkyrion (no absorb for Barriers to exploit) and Plasmafier (low BV makes high-pressure less effective).

### Punf's Unit Assessments
> Source: Punf — guest article on Yujiri's site (archived, Dec 2022)

- **Centrifuge/Chrono Filter**: Only for "cheating things" (enabling otherwise impossible purchases)
- **Endotherm Kit**: Weak without specific combo support
- **Iceblade Golem**: Go all-in or don't buy — partial investment gets exploited
- **Resophore**: Deceptively weak despite appearances
- **Steelforge**: Commonly forgotten despite being consistently strong
- **The Wincer**: Extremely good — "need a very strong reason not to buy"
- **Tia Thurnax**: Never try to match/counter with equivalent aggression
- **Zemora**: Buy turn 3-4 off a Conduit/Conduit line

## Conduit-Branch Unit Profiles
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Conduit Technology in Prismata" (Nov 2014)

### Trinity Drone — Green Economy Shell
"Devotees to green tech often find that their army is invincible, only to have their one-health Drones get slaughtered. Trinity Drone solves that problem by replacing 3 Drones with a 5 health protective shell that operates at full power until all 5 damage is dealt to it. Very few other units in the game allow you to build up a powerful economy without committing to much, if any, defense."

### Iso Kronus — Burst Synchronization
"Iso Kronus shines in games where you need extra burst damage to get past big defenses. An alternative to Gauss Cannon, Iso Kronus attacks for twice as much every other turn. You often want to make sure your Iso Kronuses are attacking at the same time, which is easy since gold and green can be stored. Be wary of your opponent picking off your Iso Kronuses on the turn before they would attack."

### Aegis — Economy Without Drone Risk
"The big green defender that doesn't consume your Drones. It's fatter than Wall, but doesn't have the absorption benefit that Walls have. Aegis can often be combined with an absorber like Wall for great value, because it's more economically efficient than buying three Forcefields."

### Gauss Fabricator — Inevitable Value
"Fabricator builds 40 health worth of units over 8 turns, which guarantees a bright future for your game once its full value kicks in. Simple and brutally efficient, the only downside is that your opponent will see it coming from miles away. But that doesn't necessarily mean that they'll be able to react in time."

### Cryo Ray — Layered Freeze
"A single Cryo Ray is an inefficient way to cancel out their defending Engineer; but once you have a couple of these, you have so many options. Freeze their biggest defender and deny them the ability to absorb damage, or freeze all their little defenders and take out their more expensive units or even legendaries. Fortunately green can be stored, so you can get a pile of these all at once."

### Venge Cannon — Late-Game Drone Conversion
"This unit turns your flimsy Drones into high-HP cannons of doom that make use of your leftover greens at the end of the game. Use the ability sparingly when your opponent is already whacking at your Venge Cannons though; it is better value to compromise the HP on your Venge Cannons while you still have Walls to protect them."

### Cluster Bolt — Calculated Burst
"Normally, paying 4 Green for this exchange of blows isn't great value, but if you have high-health Trinity Drones and Gauss Cannons, then 2 damage to you means a lot less than 5 damage to your opponent's Tarsiers. Another time to take advantage of Cluster Bolt is when your opponent has no attack and you have a Wall, since their 2 damage will only be absorbed."

### Zemora Voidbringer — Conduit Synergy
"While you're waiting for Zemora to construct, your Conduits will be working to produce future attack, which Zemora duplicates as gold for your economy. For maximum efficiency, have enough Conduits to power Zemora every turn."

### Plexo Cell — Panic Defense
"This bubbly construction combines the efficiency (and also the weaknesses) of Barrier with the ability of Forcefield to tap into your Drones. Combined with a strong absorber, it actually makes you feel like you can defend forever."

## Frostbite Defense Theory
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Defending against Frostbites in Prismata" (Elyot Grant, Jul 2015)

### The Optimal Defense Principle
"An optimal defensive play should make the attacker's decision hard."

Most beginners overdefend against Frostbites — holding back too many Drones/Steelsplitters to guarantee no breach. The optimal play is to allow a *small* breach that the opponent would consider a bad trade:

**Core insight**: "If you want to play optimally against an opponent with a huge stack of Frostbites, you should almost always be allowing your opponent a small breach. And more generally, you should always be making your opponent's decision difficult. If your opponent has an easy decision (e.g. it's 100% clear that they'll choose not to use the Frostbites), then you've probably overdefended."

**Minimax principle applied**: Work/attack with enough Drones/Steelsplitters so that the opponent views "blow Frostbites now" vs "save for later" as roughly equal. You get free extra gold/attack at no cost — because the opponent's best play (save Frostbites) is unchanged.

**Exception**: When marginal breach kills a Lucina/Centurion (game-ending), full defense is correct. The rule applies when the marginal breach value is ~1 Wall/Tarsier.

### Frost Brooder Redesign
Frost Brooder was nerfed from 5RR to 4RR but its Frostbites now have Lifespan 3. "This makes it much more difficult to store up a huge pile of Frostbites, and presents a ton of really interesting strategic questions, since your Frostbites are now 'use-em-or-lose-em'."

## Build Time as Balance Tool
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Longer Build Times" (Elyot Grant, Sep 2015)

Build time >1 serves three design purposes:
1. **Creating interesting gamestates** — planning several turns ahead when Zemora/Resophore is under construction
2. **Balance adjustment** — Tarsier was originally 5R BT1, changed to 4R BT2 to reduce dominance
3. **Enabling combos** — "Lining up purchases so everyone comes online at once"

### Xeno Guardian Build Time Change
Changed from 7BG BT1 to 5BG BT2. "Xeno Guardian exhibits the highest player 2 advantage of any of the units currently in Prismata." The BT2 change balanced P1/P2 openings, made multiple Xeno Guardians stronger in long games, and weakened the very first one as a defender.

### Scorchilla Build Time History
7RG BT1 → 5RG BT2 → 3RG BT3. The initial change dampened "the dreaded P2 DD/DDC/DA/2xScorchilla" opening (58% P2 winrate). At 3RG BT3: "allows for some really fun timing attacks (2xScorchilla into TT into RR for 10 damage, anyone?)."

## Thorium Dynamo — Developer Case Study
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Thorium Dynamo" (Elyot Grant, Jul 2015)

"This unit allows you to get a huge amount of income quickly without investing in additional Engineers." Nerf from GGG→4 gold click ability to GGG→3 gold. Original design enabled 35+ income economies without a 3rd Engineer — "too crazy."

**Design lesson**: "Thorium Dynamo, along with Tantalum Ray, are the first of a handful of new units that have been created with the knowledge gained after releasing Prismata on Kickstarter — players really like powerful economic units, enjoy interesting click abilities, and love units that bring flexibility in the use of different techs."

## Deadeye Operative — Balance History
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Changes Coming to Deadeye Operative" (Sep 2016)

Changed from 4BB 1HP to 5BB 2HP. "Deadeye is notoriously difficult to balance because its viability in a given match rests precariously on how efficient it is relative to buying Drones. Too strong, and mirror matches happen in almost every Deadeye game. Too weak, and players can't go for the unit because it gets punished simply by Droning up."

Previous iterations: 5BB 3HP (too strong defensively — rushes survived too easily) → 4BB 1HP (too cheap — sniping Drones too efficient) → 5BB 2HP (strict nerf over 3HP version, but still viable).

## May 2015 Balance Patch — Unit-Specific Insights
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Our Largest Balance Patch Ever" (Elyot Grant, May 2015)

### Militia — HP Buff (2 → 4)
"While players often bought Militias during the opening to take advantage of their gold-producing ability, Militias had little use in the late game, often being simply worse than Steelsplitters. Now, with higher HP, the decision between Militias and Steelsplitters will be more interesting later in the game."

### Hannibull — Redesign (9RB → 10RB, 6HP → 7HP, split damage)
Now does 1 damage passively + 1 more when clicked (was 0 + 2). "Weakens Hannibull's strength as a rusher by increasing its cost. Simultaneously, Hannibull's potential value later in the game is now quite a bit higher, on par with units like Chieftain."

### Cynestra — Cost Increase (11GGGR → 12GGGR)
"Cynestra, while already a bit weaker than Shadowfang in terms of pure value per unit cost, didn't result in players being punished enough for constructing it because it didn't require the tech investment of a double Animus." Previously had GRRR cost (changed to GGGR to differentiate from Shadowfang).

### Tesla Coil — Redesign (10GGB → 12GGB, comes with 3 Engineers)
"Tesla Coil is essentially an overpowered unit whose long-term value is kept in check only by limited Engineer supply, meaning that its overpoweredness really shines during the opening phase." The extra Engineer doesn't reduce total supply — a buff in long games with 3+ Coils.

### Drake — Cost Increase (16BBB → 17BBB)
"Drake was one of the most P1-favouring units in Prismata." The P1 opening DD/DDE/DDD/DDDB/WBB comfortably pumps Drakes from 16 Drones. "Drake itself is one of the strongest BBB units because the on-click ability, while not being incredibly efficient, often forces the defending player to overdefend against the threat."

### Xaetron — Attack Weakened (7HP→7 charges to 7HP→5 charges)
"Xaetron is actually an incredibly powerful unit both defensively and offensively, but we found players favouring its offensive potential more. Consequently, we modified the '1 for 1' attack ratio. Xaetron should now be much more balanced as an attacker and defender."

### Vai Mauronax — Buff (15BRRR → 14BRRR, Chill 6 → 7)
"With the ability to Chill for 7, Vai gains the ability to freeze Chieftains, Defense Grids, and the new Hannibull."

### Resophore — Complete Redesign (10GG BT1 → 1RG BT6)
"Resophore's ability doesn't really combo with any other units, so in a lot of Resophore games, it's either optimal to not get Resophore at all, or to get Resophores only." The BT6 redesign forces players to "play a game of Prismata before the Resophore itself becomes available." Red in the cost ensures Tarsiers see play before Forcefield spam.

### Corpus — Cost Increase (9RRR → 10RRR)
"Corpus + Shadowfang rushes were extremely strong (possibly broken), so a nerf to 10RRR was warranted."

### The Wincer — Cost Decrease (10GBBR → 9GBBR)
"The Wincer is a very extreme unit — the biggest example of burst damage in Prismata. This makes it quite resistant to all of our usual mathy methods of evaluating it."

### Cluster Bolt — Damage Reduction (5/2 → 4/1)
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Cluster Bolt is going to be changed" (Apr 2015)

Changed from 5 Gauss Charges for you / 2 for opponent to 4/1. "In situations where the 2 damage 'backswing' was of low impact (absorbed by a Wall or only damaging high HP units), Cluster Bolt offers too much value." Cluster Bolt all-ins "will definitely still be a thing, especially with Venge Cannon around, but counterplay should be more effective."

**Solving concern**: "Players could study sets like [Base + Cluster Bolt + Venge Cannon] to the point that they were completely solved. These two units together occur in about 1% of Prismata games."

## Scorchilla — Comprehensive Combo Guide
> Source: Redrame — "A Guide to Using Scorchilla" (Reddit r/Prismata, 2015)

### Why Scorchilla Appears Weak
"Scorchilla in its current state is almost exclusively a combo unit. When the best few of these combos are available, Scorchilla is actually significantly above average." Its apparent weakness comes from:
1. **Top units counter it**: Alt drones (Vivid, Doomed, Wild, Trinity) promote high-econ games where Scorchilla is weaker
2. **Doomed Mech and Chieftain** are incredibly strong and can be timed to interrupt burst turns
3. **Dominant absorbers** (Defense Grid) make synced Scorchilla absorb-denial a waste

### Scorchilla Combo Partners
- **Tia Thurnax**: The classic combo. Tia's burst combined with Scorchilla sync is devastating.
- **Protoplasm**: Strong in low-econ Scorchilla sets.
- **Frostbite/freeze**: "Way too good with freeze."

### Key Tactical Principle
"You basically need a fairly early quad Scorchilla turn as a lead-off in high econ, or else the pressure doesn't usually matter enough." In low-econ games, even 2 Scorchillas from a single Animus creates immediate 6 burst pressure.

## Vivid Drone — P1/P2 Balance Study
> Source: Lunarch Studios Blog — "Vivid Drone, Unit Stats" + "Vivid Drone Revamp" (Elyot Grant, 2015-2016)

"Vivid Drone currently exhibits the highest player imbalance among all units in Prismata. When Vivid Drone is present, the P1 winrate is 54.4%."

**Root cause is NOT unit imbalance**: "Player 1's advantage is almost entirely due to player 2 having more opening choices, and consequently choosing a suboptimal choice more often. The winrate gap shrinks away almost entirely when only considering games featuring top Tier X players."

**Design dilemma**: "Often, when balancing a unit, we have to choose between: (1) Balance that leads to a 50% winrate, (2) Balance that leads to both players having equal winning chances if they both play optimal openings." Units like Vivid Drone where P1 has few opening choices but P2 has many make these two goals conflict.

**Eventual redesign**: Original 4EEE Vivid Drone was completely replaced with a new unit design (10 supply, different mechanics). The original version was preserved as a campaign-only unit with new name and art.

## Alternate Drone Design Philosophy
> Source: Allroc + community discussion — Reddit r/Prismata (2015)

"Autowin or heavily advantaged sets cannot truly exist when natural tech openings are disrupted by alternate econ/tech."

**Doomed Drone** as game balance tool: "I feel like I can go for any strategy as any player when that unit is in the set, and it's even inherently good against cheesy linear rushes."

**Auric Impulse** as high skill-cap unit: "Incredibly high skill cap unit that both facilitates rushes and helps to counter them."

**Community proposal**: Some players advocated adding Doomed Drone or Auric Impulse to the base set to improve game balance and reduce the impact of problematic random sets.

## Kinetic Driver — Community Analysis
> Source: roit_ + community discussion — Reddit r/Prismata (2015)

"This unit is superbly designed. It's a unit that requires you to make a hard decision when it's on the board." The decision lies on two axes: **when to fire** (early for tempo vs late for maximum attack value) and **which building to hit** (Blastforge to slow absorb vs Animus to delay attack).

**Example play**: "Sniped a Conduit two turns in to prevent my opponent from buying Cynestra, which forced him to buy Tarsiers with his Animus and allowed me to get the first Cynestra."

**Design insight**: "The complexity of most units comes from timing when to buy them. But Kinetic Driver is different because it creates an interesting multidimensional on-the-board decision."

## Scorchilla Clicking and Syncing
> Source: amalloy — "WDIL: Msven vs Kolento" (YouTube)

### Scorchilla Click Value Rule of Thumb
"A Scorch clicked every time is worth 1 attack on average (3 attack / 3 turns). Holding a Scorch to deny 1 absorb is roughly neutral." The real value of Scorchillas comes from syncing them to burst through absorb barriers.

### Scorch Syncing Against Doomed Wall
Having Doomed Walls against synced Scorchillas is a liability: "The lifespan becomes a problem because you know exactly when your defense will drop." Scorchillas synced to fire the turn after Doomed Walls expire can generate devastating exploits.

### Scorchillas as Absorb-Denial Timing Units
> Source: sileneundulata — "League Game Analysis: WDIL with sileneundulata" (YouTube)

"It's best to think of Scorchillas in the same way you think of Tarsiers — anything with build time, built before you have or are already over the absorb barrier, is preventing your opponent from getting value from their absorb." Scorch's 3-turn build time means the opponent can't immediately start absorbing.

"Scorches generally aren't a good pressure unit — a Scorch provides strictly less pressure than an Electrovore. The value of sinking attack is that your opponent has to buy prompt defense a turn before."

### Scorch Holding Heuristic (n sub n)
> Source: sileneundulata — "League Game Analysis: WDIL with sileneundulata" (YouTube)

"According to n sub n, you should hold Scorches if you've got more coming in and your number of Scorches is less than or equal to the amount of absorb they've got. That's about the break-even point."

## Xaetron + Energy Matrix Absorb Rotation
> Source: amalloy — "WDIL: NativeAvian vs KP" (YouTube)

"Alternate absorbing between Xaetron and something else. If you always block on Matrix, absorb 4/turn. If you alternate, you absorb 4 on Matrix turns AND gain 4 health from Xaetron healing on those turns." The rotation generates strictly more total value than absorbing on either alone.

**Design implication**: Large absorbers that heal (Xaetron) benefit from being paired with a secondary absorber and alternating, rather than always absorbing on the largest available.

## Deadeye Operative: P2 Economy Rule
> Source: amalloy — "WDIL: String vs razor1n" (YouTube)

"As P2 in a Deadeye set, don't go 3rd Engineer because you run out of Drone supply too quickly." Deadeye sets require careful Drone management — the 20 Drone supply limit becomes relevant when both players are droning up. P2 starting with 7 Drones has less room to expand.

### Savior Timing in Deadeye Sets
"Savior is a mid-game purchase, not an early one." In Deadeye sets, buying Savior too early wastes its doubling ability because you don't have enough Drones to sacrifice. Wait until you have 14+ Drones and can leverage the Drone-sacrifice mechanic.

### Xeno Guardian vs Freeze
"Xeno Guardian is excellent against freeze because it has 5 HP and is fragile — it doesn't care about being frozen since it's not absorbing anyway." High-HP fragile units naturally counter chill strategies because their HP provides soak value regardless of freeze status.

## Nivo Charge as Game-Deciding Unit
> Source: amalloy — "WDIL: Apooche vs PinkTeddy" (YouTube)

In Mahar Rectifier breachproof games, "Nivo Charge is the game-decider." Its ability to add burst damage at key moments can overwhelm a breachproof defense that otherwise has no crack. The unit's presence in the set should strongly influence whether breachproof is viable.

## Defense Grid vs Xaetron Interaction
> Source: amalloy — "WDIL: Leary93 vs sigmasin" (YouTube)

### Don't Stack Defense Grid + Xaetron
"Defense Grid does not synergize with Xaetron as well as some other big absorbers do. With Wall, averaging 2 with 8 is great — it gets you 5 absorb per turn on average. Whereas alternating Defense Grid and Xaetron only gets you 1 extra absorb per turn, and you have to go quite far out of your way."

### Thermite Core Punishes Defense Grid
"Thermite Core is just disgusting against Defense Grid — you can deny absorb completely for multiple turns in a row for a ridiculous cost." The counter: build up permanent attack slowly via Thermite Cores, denying Grid absorb while accumulating Pixie damage. Don't rush for Defense Grid when Thermite Cores are in the set.

## Redeemer Post-Nerf: Soak Dependency
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

After the nerf, Redeemer "tends to rely on having excellent soak units to be good, whereas it used to just sort of be generically good." The key shift: Redeemer's value is now conditional on having units like Plexo Cell or other high-HP soak available. Without strong soak, Redeemer underperforms its stat line. In sets where Plexo Cell is present alongside Redeemer, "Plexo Cell just makes Redeemer good" — the soak quality enables Redeemer's theoretical efficiency to materialize.

## Doom Wall Cycling Cost in Shrinking Economies
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

Doom Wall's 3-turn lifespan creates a hidden endgame weakness: "every other turn you have to have a plan for getting a new one." Against permanent absorbers like Centurion or Energy Matrix, Doom Wall becomes increasingly expensive to maintain as economies shrink. In late-game positions where gold is tight, the recurring 5G cost to replace expiring Doom Walls can become unsustainable, forcing sub-optimal defense allocation.

## Manticore vs Non-Granular Defenses
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

"Non-granular defenses kind of get punished by Manticore." The 4-damage attack creates awkward defense math for opponents with only large HP defenders — they either over-defend (wasting resources) or under-defend (losing units). Sets with Manticore should push players toward acquiring granular defense (Engineers, Forcefields, small soak) rather than relying solely on Walls or Infusion Grids.

## Trinity Drone Endgame Tradeoffs
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

"Trinities can be pretty dang good in end games where your attackers are like three or four health" — their 5 HP absorbs multiple hits from mid-sized attackers, making them efficient soak. However, "in a set with Plexo Cell the advantage probably just goes to having all your drones sacrificeable" — Plexo Cell's soak mechanic prefers regular Drones (1 HP, freely sacrificeable) over Trinity Drones (5 HP, can't be partially sacrificed). The presence of Plexo Cell specifically reduces Trinity Drone's appeal even in breachproof-adjacent strategies.

## Endotherm Kit + Thermite Core Combo
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

"Kit also excellent with Thermite Core. You keep clicking your Thermites — that forces your opponent to put out a bunch of defense... then you freeze it all and pop your pixies for breach damage." The synergy works because Thermite Core creates pressure that forces defensive commitment, then Kit's freeze denies that committed defense, allowing the accumulated Pixie damage to punch through. This two-phase strategy (build pressure with Thermites, then exploit with Kit freeze) is stronger than either unit alone because the opponent faces a dilemma: defend against the Pixie swarm (gets frozen) or hold back defense (gets breached by Pixies anyway).

### Colossus as Tempo Defender
"15 gold and resources for 8 health... more tempo than buying a bunch of engineers and quite a bit more value." Colossus is best understood not as a primary absorber but as a tempo play — it provides immediate bulk defense in a single purchase, freeing up future turns for attack investment. The non-prompt nature means it must be planned one turn ahead, but the resource efficiency beats piecemeal Engineer purchases.

## Asteri Cannon Click Decision Timing
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

Asteri Cannon's click ability (sacrifice for 3 burst damage) should be evaluated against remaining attack per turn: "When you have Asteri Cannons and the question is when do you click them?" The answer depends on whether 3 burst now exceeds the 1/turn sustained value over remaining game turns. In practice, click when: (1) it forces a breach this turn, (2) the game is ending within 2-3 turns anyway, or (3) opponent's defense configuration makes 3 burst damage disproportionately impactful (e.g., exactly enough to kill a key absorber).

## Fission Turret + Lance Tooth Synergy
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

In sets with both Fission Turret and Lance Tooth, the green generation from clicking dying Fission Turrets funds Lance Tooth activations. Apooche noted the Chieftain + Fission Turret + Lance Tooth triangle creates complex value chains where absorb (Chieftain), attack (Fission Turret), and absorb denial (Lance Tooth) all feed each other. The key decision point: "Do I want Chieftain or do I want Tesla Coil? Am I going aggressive... or am I going for the Chieftain, excellent defensive unit, high option value, really flexible?"

## Chieftain Absorb Chain Value
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

Chieftain's 1/turn absorb-chain mechanic rewards early purchase and long games. The cumulative value compounds — a Chieftain bought 10 turns before the game ends provides 10 absorb, equivalent to soaking an entire Wall's worth of damage spread over time. In sets without strong burst damage, Chieftain's option value (flexible between offense and defense) makes it a safe early investment regardless of which strategy the opponent commits to.

## Xaetron as Anti-Absorb-Denial Champion
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

"Xaetron is like your friend in this set... the unit that says hey, I'm here to help you deal with Thermite Core, Arka Sodara, Nivo Charge, Oxide Mixer." In sets heavy with absorb denial (freezing, clicking, or destroying defensive units), Xaetron's massive 10 HP and non-reliance on absorb makes it the natural counter-pick. It cannot be meaningfully denied — freeze only delays its damage, and its HP survives most burst attempts. When Nivo Charge threatens to fork legendary absorbers, Xaetron sidesteps the problem entirely by not caring about absorb at all.

## Blood Pact Weakness Against Breachproof
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

"Blood Pact versus breachproof... just not good enough to make up for having such low attack, because when your attack isn't enough to take out all of the Gauss charges... it can get really dangerous." Blood Pact's low base attack (1 per turn) means it struggles to generate enough pressure against Venge Cannon breachproof builds. The breachproof player's Gauss Charges survive each turn and keep attacking, while Blood Pact's supply eventually runs out. Critical insight: Blood Pact works best against standard defense, not against breachproof where total attack volume matters more than sustained pressure.

### Blood Pact Supply Exhaustion as Turning Point
When Blood Pact supply runs out, the game dynamic shifts dramatically: "that's the turning point... when they run out of Blood Pact supply, suddenly the pressure just evaporates." Commentary should flag Blood Pact remaining supply count as a key game-state variable in any match involving Blood Pact strategies.

## Grimbotch Breach Targeting: Kill Gauss Charges First
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

During breach against a Grimbotch player: "when there's a three on them, that means they're attacking you two more times, so yeah you just kill Gauss charges" — prioritize killing Gauss Charges (which attack every turn indefinitely) over Grimbotches (which only have 2-3 attacks remaining). The math: a Grimbotch with 3 stamina deals 6 more damage total; a Gauss Charge deals 1 damage per turn indefinitely. In any game lasting more than 6 turns after breach, killing the Gauss Charge is strictly better.

## Nivo Charge Fork Against Legendary Absorbers
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

"The Nivos will get very heavy pressure because all of the absorbers are like legendary or important absorbers... you have to play around forks." In sets where the main absorbers are legendary (1 supply each) — like Defense Grid, Centurion, or Xaetron — Nivo Charge's freeze creates devastating fork threats. The opponent can only have one of each, so freezing any single absorber denies their entire absorb for a turn. With multiple Nivo Charges, the attacker can threaten different absorbers simultaneously, creating positions where the defender cannot protect all options.

### Nivo + Arka + Thermite: Triple Absorb Denial
In Game 5, the set contained Nivo Charge, Arka Sodara, AND Thermite Core — three separate absorb denial mechanisms. Apooche noted this creates "absorb denial from three different angles," forcing the defender to solve an impossible puzzle: defend against freeze (Nivo), click damage (Arka), and Pixie accumulation (Thermite) simultaneously. Xaetron was identified as the only viable response because its massive HP pool absorbs punishment from all three denial types without relying on the absorb mechanic itself.

## Blood Rager Rush into Bombardier Transition
> Source: Apooche — "Aetherllama vs Allecto League Semifinal" (Twitch)

In Game 6, Aetherllama demonstrated a Blood Rager rush followed by Bombardier transition. The strategy: open with aggressive Blood Ragers for early pressure (exploiting their efficiency at 0-1 real damage), then transition into Bombardier for sustained long-range attack once the opponent is forced into defensive spending. "Blood Rager early, Bombardier for the follow-through" — the sequencing matters because Blood Rager's efficiency drops as total attack rises, while Bombardier's value increases with positional advantage.

### Electrovore + Nitrocybe Defensive Shell
In the same game, the defensive transition involved Electrovores and Nitrocybes as a red-based defensive shell. Electrovore's 4 HP for 2R and Nitrocybe's red sink utility provide efficient defense that doesn't require blue or green tech, enabling all-red aggression strategies that still have defensive options.

## Iceblade Golem — Timing and Counter-Play
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Iceblade Golem must be deployed as fast as possible to build pressure: "They have to come out kind of as fast as possible otherwise they just don't build pressure fast enough." Delayed Iceblades lose their window because the opponent has time to establish economy and defense that renders the freeze irrelevant.

### Killing Iceblades is Almost Always Losing
Using stamina attackers to kill Iceblades is a losing proposition: "Killing Iceblades is usually equivalent to admitting you've lost." The math rarely works out — Iceblades have 4 HP, so spending 4+ attack to remove 1 freeze per turn is wildly inefficient compared to buying defense to absorb the freeze.

### Polywall as Iceblade Counter
Polywall is specifically strong against Iceblade Golem: "Takes three of them to freeze it and like six of them to kill it." Polywall's 6 HP and non-fragile nature means the Iceblade player needs an enormous fleet just to neutralize one defensive unit, completely changing the economics of the matchup.

### Steelforge as Critical Breach Target Against Iceblades
In Iceblade games, Steelforge becomes the highest-priority breach target because "not only shuts off Iceblade but it shuts off Polywall and Wall." Destroying the opponent's blue production dismantles both their offensive freeze plan AND their defensive infrastructure simultaneously.

## Zamora — Base Set Matchup Analysis
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Zamora vs Tarsier in base set is "super close" — both 307th and Aetherllama had analyzed the matchup heavily. The Zamora player needs to play "really accurately" because small mistakes compound. Zamora's strength is constant pressure without consuming supply, but the Tarsier player compensates with flexibility and the ability to diversify attack timings. Apooche noted that even at the highest level, this matchup remains razor-thin.

### Zamora's Vulnerability to Savior
Savior specifically counters Zamora: "Savior does seem strong against Zamora" because Savior arrives before Zamora fires and generates enough gold via Defense Grid clicks to hold indefinitely with Walls and Engineers. The Zamora player commits early to green tech that doesn't pressure the Savior player before the Savior's economic engine stabilizes.

### Zamora + Savior Counter-Strategy
Against Zamora, the Savior player's plan is: "You pretty much always click every Plasma Fire you make and you just win — the Savior just wins the game for you on its own." The key is Defense Grid providing absorb while Savior generates gold. However, Zamora + Savior (both sides buying their respective legendaries) creates "always interesting games" where the positioning battle becomes about whether the Zamora player can pressure before the Savior stabilizes.

## Wincer — Set-Dependent Evaluation
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Wincer performs well in "high-econ high-absorb sets — really good usually." Its value scales with available economy since higher gold totals mean more clicks per turn. In sets where games go long and economies grow large, Wincer's gold-to-damage conversion outpaces traditional attackers.

### Xaetron, Hannibull, and Plexo Cell as Wincer Counters
Three units are specifically identified as "really excellent versus Wincer": Xaetron (massive HP pool shrugs off Wincer's moderate per-turn damage), Hannibull (absorb denial disrupts the defensive shell that Wincer games rely on), and Plexo Cell (cheap soak absorbs Wincer shots efficiently, maintaining the defender's economy).

### Steelforge + Wincer Synergy
Steelforge pairs naturally with Wincer: "Usually you aren't firing like an exact multiple of five drones... you just feed the rest to Steelforge." The leftover gold after Wincer clicks (which require multiples of 5) gets efficiently converted into blue defense, eliminating waste.

## Thunderhead — Guaranteed Value
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Even if Thunderhead dies immediately after its first attack, it provides "still better value than anything else." The 11-damage burst on its first turn exceeds what most attackers produce over multiple turns. This means opponents cannot ignore Thunderhead even briefly — they must either kill it before its attack turn or accept massive damage.

### Thunderhead's Guaranteed First Turn
"The Thunderhead's always getting one turn because if you respond to Thunderhead with Isochronon, the Isos don't attack right away." Even reactive counter-play (buying Isochronons to reach 11 damage and kill Thunderhead) arrives too late — Thunderhead attacks first. The only way to prevent Thunderhead's first turn of value is to already have 11+ damage prepared before the opponent buys it.

### Thunderhead vs Blood Phage + Flame Animus
Thunderhead "stops it cold" against Blood Phage + Flame Animus strategies because "there's no way to make enough damage against that" — the red-based attack package lacks the burst potential to reach 11 damage needed to kill Thunderhead before it fires repeatedly. Without a Clusterbolt-like unit to combine damage, the Blood Phage player cannot answer Thunderhead.

"Centrifuge turns are impossible" — Centrifuge creates extremely complex decision trees because each turn involves selecting which resource to generate, and the optimal choice depends on future turn plans that themselves depend on Centrifuge choices. ## Vai Mauronax — Synergy with Arka Sodara
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Vai Mauronax is strengthened by Arka Sodara's presence in the set. The combination of Vai's massive attack with Arka's absorb denial creates overwhelming pressure that most defensive setups cannot handle. "If you can get both you probably win" — the dual threat of burst damage (Vai) and absorb denial (Arka) leaves defenders without good options.

### Vai Alone Doesn't Guarantee Victory
"Vai Mauronax alone doesn't guarantee a win because you still have the potential to go Forge into Synthesizer to draw" — the old Synthesizer draw-out remains a theoretical escape valve. Getting Vai is dominant but not unconditionally winning.

### Centurion vs Vai: Counter-Play Timing
In Wild Drone + Centurion + Vai sets, timing is critical: "You often want Vai once they set up to buy Centurion — it should be value." Buy Vai reactively when the opponent commits to Centurion infrastructure, not preemptively: "You don't want to buy it like well before they're even preparing to get Centurion." Also critical: "You probably don't want to throw that stuff down until your opponent can't respond with a Tia threat."

## Vivid Drone — Defensive Vulnerability

## Savior — Balance and Tempo Analysis
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Savior's balance change (from an earlier version) prevented "certain early Savior builds from just auto winning on turn two" but created new dynamics: "Savior commits second compared to a lot of things that it beats like Zamora, so it's kind of funny where it gave it more space to be beating Zamora." The increased build time allows the Savior player to see the opponent's commitment before investing.

### Defense Grid + Savior Economy
"Defense Grid is just so insane — what are you spending your gold on? Basically Walls, Engineers, Rhinos... you pretty much always click every Plasma Fire you make and you just win." Defense Grid's absorb combined with Savior's gold generation creates a self-sustaining defensive economy. The Savior player's game plan simplifies to: maintain absorb, click Plasma Fires, buy walls.

### Early Breach Against Savior is Survivable
"I've had quite a few games where my Savior showed up, I got breached for one or two drones, but the amount my opponent paid for it meant I won anyways." Savior's economic engine is strong enough to absorb early breaches — the opponent's investment in pressure often costs more than the damage they inflict.

## Isochronon — Context for Buying Over Asteri Cannon
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Three factors favor Isochronon over Asteri Cannon in certain sets: (1) a larger absorber exists, making the deny-absorb-every-other-turn effect of Isochronon more impactful; (2) Thunderhead is in the set, making burst potential critical for reaching 11 damage; (3) Nivo Charge threat makes Isochronon's burst valuable for surprise damage. "If you kind of go big enough, all of those things stop mattering and an Asteri Cannon is indeed better — by quite a lot." The decision is tempo-sensitive: Iso for early/mid advantage, Asteri for late-game grinding.

## Nivo Charge — Trickling vs Burst
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

Trickling Nivo Charges (buying one per turn) has a significant downside: "I kind of don't love the trickling of Nivos because then you run out of supply to keep the pressure up for multiple turns." A single Nivo often only forces a Corpus or small defensive buy (3 pressure, 1 damage) rather than truly threatening the opponent's position. Saving multiple Nivos for a burst turn creates a much larger strategic threat that can end games.

### Nivo vs Infusion Grid Math
"Buying fewer Nivos than Infusion Grids that your opponent has" is problematic. Two Infusion Grids absorb 10 freeze, requiring 3+ Nivos to meaningfully pressure. Single Nivo against double Infusion Grid provides "barely pressure — it's doing a damage" and little else. Scale Nivo purchases to match or exceed the opponent's Infusion Grid count.

## Fission Turret — Wild Drone Interaction
> Source: Apooche, "6 Player Round Robin Tournament" (Twitch VOD, ~2017)

In Wild Drone sets, early Fission Turret builds enable killing Wild Drones sooner than the opponent, creating an economy differential. Apooche noted that Aetherllama's "early Fission build to kill Wilds earlier in the game" was "pulling a decent bit ahead in economy" — killing the opponent's Wilds while preserving your own creates a compounding advantage.
