## Unit Efficiency Analysis (Mathematical)
> Source: Yujiri (former #2 ranked player) — "Intro to the Math Behind Prismata" + "Math Data" (yujiri.xyz, archived)

### Inflation Theory
"Inflation" = how much more valuable something is now vs next turn. With Drones: 9 gold now → 12 gold next turn (3 Drones + 3 gold produced) = **1.33x inflation**. Higher inflation means faster units (prompt, short buildtime) are relatively better.

**Resource values** (derived from tech building balance):
- Gold = 1.00, Green = 1.33, Blue = 1.67, Red = 1.00, Energy ≈ 0.56

**Barrier Value (BV)**: How much gold 1 point of defense costs. Base set BV ≈ 2.3 (Wall-based).

### Green-Blue Attacker Comparison
> Source: Yujiri — "Compares the Green-Blue Attackers in Prismata"

Four comparable green-blue pressure attackers ranked by efficiency:
1. **Plasmafier** (1.54) — "By far the most efficient. Drone saccing makes you overteched but it's quite worth it." Also blocks when it doesn't attack.

### Immolite vs Tarsier Decision Tree
> Source: Yujiri — "When to Buy Immolite versus Tarsier"

1. If Tarsier's buildtime can deny absorb → **buy Tarsier**
2. If Immolite's finesse exploits the enemy → **buy Immolite**
3. If Immolite lets you spend tech you'd otherwise waste → **buy Immolite**
4. If Immolite doesn't force prompt defense → **buy Tarsier**
5. If Immolite forces efficient defense (Aegis, Polywall) → **probably Tarsier**
6. If Immolite forces medium defense (Wall) → **probably Immolite**
7. If Immolite forces inefficient defense (Forcefield or worse) → **buy Immolite**

- **Gauss Fabricator** — "Good at rushing for same reason as Tarsier (delayed attack), only much more efficient. Green can be floated."

"Super greedy attackers with a unique feel":
- **Tatsu Nullifier** — "Hard-counters almost every big defender: Energy Matrix, Doomed Wall, Defense Grid, Centurion, Plexo Cell"

## Absorber Rankings
> Source: 307th — "Absorber Rankings" (prismatalibrary.blog, Feb 2018)

Ranked on three criteria: **value** (damage nullification efficiency), **convenience** (tech/build requirements), **clunkiness** (difficulty obtaining full value).

### S Tier
- **Centurion** — Highest value absorber. Prompt and reliable. Rarely skipped unless competing against powerful Big Red units. Tech: Green-Blue-Red.

### A Tier
- **Defense Grid** — Second-strongest but requires three Blue tech. Vulnerable to Chill. Benefits from Blue sinks.
- **Energy Matrix** — "Really good value, convenient, and reliable." 4 absorb/turn at 8BB cost.
- **Colossus** — Third-strongest by value but extremely clunky. Fragile with 4 supply limit; requires 7 damage/turn for full absorption.
- **Xeno Guardian** — Vigilant: attacks while defending. Good value with reliability.

### B Tier
- **Infusion Grid** — Convenient blue absorber, superior to Wall. Bridge to larger absorbers.
- **Bombarder** — Strong overall; best absorption at 1 stamina when attacking provides no pressure.
- **Urban Sentry** — Vigilant Blue-Green. Excellent first attacker despite slightly weaker value than Infusion Grid.
- **Omega Splitter** — Better value than Infusion Grid but prohibitively expensive (15BBB).
- **Doomed Wall** — Similar to Infusion Grid. 3-turn lifespan creates Chill vulnerability.
- **Xaetron** — Fragile but gains 4 health/turn regardless of damage. Excels against Chill through threat manipulation.
- **Doomed Mech** — Comparable value to Xaetron. Lifespan creates Chill vulnerability.
- **Mahar Rectifier** — Permanent fragile unit requiring two copies for reliable rotation.
- **Feral Warden** — Red-Green absorber. Stronger early, loses value as games progress.
- **Odin** — Primarily attacker; worth buying before opponent threatens damage due to 4 threat value.
- **Chieftain** — Primarily soak unit; works as absorber by timing lifespan correctly.

### C Tier
- **Protoplasm** — Weak early, strong late-game. Opposite of Feral Warden.
- **Wall** — Convenient but weak. Default when no better absorber exists.

### Rhino Tier
- **Rhino** — Pure Red absorber. Weakest option due to Red's aggressive nature.

### Special Category
- **Arka Sodara** — Insanely high value under right circumstances, but costs 7 attack. Creates paradox where both players avoid buying it first, giving decisive advantage to second Arka buyer. Does NOT affect economy sizing like standard absorbers.

**Commentary insight**: Economy size correlates with absorber strength — better absorbers enable larger economies. Absorbers providing attack (Centurion, Xeno Guardian, Odin) reward aggressive play alongside defense.

## Noticeable Units (Expert Analysis)
> Source: Foxclear — Advanced Strategy Guide: Noticeable Units (wiki)

Units that cannot be ignored and will shape any set they appear in.

### Centurion — The Strongest Unit
"Currently, the strongest unit in the game, and it is really rare that I will not go for it." Even worth buying when opponent can freeze it (like against Vai Mauronax), because of the high defense tempo and aggression it provides. Occasionally, ultra-aggressive sets won't let you buy it, but "most often, Centurion tames even the angry Red stuff."

### Tia Thurnax — The Highest Impact
"The unit with the highest immediate impact." A "naked Tia" (without other attackers) rarely succeeds, but it must be bought during the game. **Timing is critical**: higher economy = buy later. "In a fast, aggressive game with Wall as best absorber, a quick Tia along with 2 Tarsiers can often get the job done."

### Thunderhead — The Anti-Aggression
"Provided there is no burst damage available (Gaussite Symbiote, Cluster Bolt), Thunderhead will make any aggressive strategy crumble." Even staying 2 turns is quite valuable. Before buying, evaluate the maximum damage your opponent can get to shut it down: "if it is equal to 11 or more, think again!" **Amazing synergy with Redeemer**: buying Redeemer right after Thunderhead creates massive pressure; opponent's Gauss Charges crash into Thunderhead on its last lifespan turn.

### Xaetron — The Anti-Abuse
Best unit against any kind of defensive abuse — it regenerates health regardless of damage patterns. Key tips:
- Buy it against 0-attack strategies (synched Iso Kronus or Scorchillas)
- Never click unless opponent isn't attacking it (reaching 12 health) or you desperately need pressure
- **Dual-absorber strategy**: Absorb on Xaetron one turn (when high health), overdefend Xaetron next turn to absorb on backup absorber. With Wall: averages 5 absorb (vs 4 on Xaetron alone). With stronger absorbers: even better.

### The Wincer — The Biggest Cannon
Highest damage output from a single unit. "The pressure can usually not be defended in one turn, and the first shot rarely gets there. But the second shot is usually the strong one." **Key insight**: you can HOLD The Wincer — the pressure alone is often enough. "If it is defended with frontline units (Polywall, Hannibull), try to set up exact damage with permanent attackers and hold The Wincer while taking down the frontline." Against 1-lifespan defense (Plexo Cell), don't shoot — let the defense expire in vain.

### Arka Sodara — The Largest Value
"Whenever Arka Sodara is in the set, you practically have to get at some point the 7 attack threshold with BBR tech." Counter-Arka (buying your own Arka with exactly 7 damage to deny the 6 absorb) is well-known but "do not overestimate the counter-Arka — getting Arka first means you get to click it first." The concept loses its point as damage exceeds 7. **Trap**: Be careful with R sinks like Perforator or Tyranno Smorcus — attacking with them spends the Red needed to buy Arka.

### Thermite Core — The Most Annoying
"Recently buffed, this unit is difficult to deal with. With the right set-up, you can deny absorb completely multiple turns in a row for a ridiculous cost." **Against it**: defend for exact every turn without using high-value absorbers while Thermites have full value, or go breachproof. **Playing it**: aim for even attack values matching 2× your Thermite Core count. Odd values reduce Thermite value (building only 1 Pixie instead of 2).

## Unit-Specific Tactical Patterns
> Source: User-provided research (Prismata blog, community analysis)

### Odin — Full Build Notation & Timing
**Standard Odin build (P1)**: DD/DDE/DDD/DDDB/DDD/BBB+Odin (costs 20G+3B, comes with 3 Steelsplitters, click to sacrifice a Splitter for 4 attack).

**P2 Turn 8 Gold Storage**: P2 can buy Odin by storing gold on Turn 7 (floating ~4 gold) to have exactly 20G+3B on Turn 8. Creates an earlier Odin than standard builds.

**Three equilibrium exceptions** where Odin isn't worth buying despite appearing dominant:
1. **Cynestra + Pixie** — Cynestra assassinates Odin's Steelsplitters, denying click value
2. **Wincer cliff** — Wincer's burst can overwhelm Odin's defense before it generates enough value
3. **Old Drake** (pre-nerf) — Drake's Blastforge sacrifice produces enough threat to outpace Odin

**Nerf history**: Odin nerfed from 5HP to 3HP. "Odin is the most player-1-favouring unit in high-level Prismata play." The nerf made it more vulnerable to breach targeting.

### Protoplasm — Universally Strong
"Protoplasm is always good if no one has blue answers." Without blue tech (no Wall, no Forcefield), Protoplasm's growth is unchecked. The community considers it universally strong in sets lacking blue-based defense.

### Scorchilla Rush — Over-Selected by P2
P2 Double Scorchilla rush is over-selected because it "looks scary but falls apart against Wall." Efficient defense (Wall absorbs the burst, Engineers handle off-turns) nullifies the pressure. Only strong when Wall supply is contested or opponent lacks blue.

### Trinity Drone — Breach-Tolerant Glue
Beyond breachproof economy, Trinity Drone acts as "breach-tolerant glue" — it continues producing green after breach (unlike Conduit, which isn't targeted but whose green goes to waste without economy). Enables spare green conversion to Forcefields during emergencies.

### Kinetic Driver — Defense Reducer via Tech-Sniping
Kinetic Driver's unique value is as a "defense reducer" — it snipes tech buildings (Blastforges, Animuses) to deny future defensive purchases. The timing window is critical: snipe early enough to prevent absorber setup, late enough to have gathered sufficient attack. "The best Kinetic Driver targets are tech buildings the opponent is counting on for their defensive plan."

### Nivo Charge — Burst Pattern
Nivo Charge enables a "stored green" burst pattern: accumulate green over 2-3 turns, then buy 2-3 Nivos simultaneously for massive freeze pressure. The burst forces either a breach or a massively inefficient defense response. However, "if your opponent can defend it, his defense will stick around for the following turns, so you have to rebuy the same amount of Nivos" — and supply is scarce.

### Ebb Turbine — ~2 Turn Payback
Ebb Turbine pays back its gold cost in approximately 2 turns, making it one of the fastest-returning economic investments. However, its value is heavily set-dependent — it needs good blue sinks to justify the slot.

### Wincer Rush Shell
Standard Wincer build: DD/DDC/AB/D1 forces P1 into DDE response. The Wincer threat alone shapes the entire game: "even unclicked, The Wincer is doing work because your opponent must prepare defense for the potential shot."

### Iceblade Golem — Draw Potential
Iceblade Golem games have a 0.98% draw rate — the highest of any unit. This is because Iceblade's high HP (7) and freeze ability create stalemate-prone endgames where neither player can break through.

### Plexo Cell — Confirmed Fragile
Plexo Cell has the Fragile keyword, meaning damage to it is permanent. This is sometimes overlooked but is crucial for evaluation — it means Plexo Cell cannot absorb, only soak.

## Balance Change History
> Source: Prismata Blog — Balance Patches + Developer analysis

| Unit | Change | Date | Rationale |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| Odin | 5HP → 3HP | Jan 2019 | "Most P1-favouring unit." Experiments showed lower HP still playable. |
| Wild Drone | Redesigned entirely | Jan 2019 | "An entirely new unit with completely different synergies." |
| Militia | Buffed (dynamics) | Jan 2019 | "Top player Msven championed this as a way of making the unit more dynamic." |
| Redeemer | No change (confirmed) | Jan 2019 | "Some players suggested P2 bias, but statistics showed little to no bias." |
| Vendium Scrap | Design exploration | 2018 | Designed to create more openings; Vendium Scrap lets P1 access P2-exclusive builds. |

**Design philosophy**: "Rather than simply hitting obvious outliers with nerfs, we took a more nuanced approach — adjusting units that created undesirable patterns while also buffing units that were underused."

**Combat removal philosophy**: The developers intentionally avoided unit-on-unit direct combat. All damage goes through the pooled attack/defense system. This prevents "rock-paper-scissors at the unit level" and ensures strategic depth comes from army composition and timing, not individual unit matchups.
