# Community Jargon (Manual Review Required)

These items need manual review for tone before any integration.

### "GF'd" (Greatly Favored) means the opponent has a significant advantage that approaches an autowin; players often overstate winrate implications when using this term.
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- consensus (2018-04)

### Base set units are commonly abbreviated by their in-game hotkey: F = Forcefield (not FF which is ambiguous for two Forcefields); this notation is standard in replays and analysis.
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- p0lari, steel0229e (2018-04)

### In Prismata notation, lowercase letters (r, g, b) denote resource types (red, green, blue) and uppercase letters (R, G, B) denote units that produce those resources. Gold is typically written with a standalone number (e.g., '2' means 2 gold), and can optionally be labeled as '2 gold' for clarity to new players. Capital G is reserved for Gaussite.
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- mrguy888, apooche (2018-05-11)

### 'Splashing a color' means buying a small amount of one color in a build dominated by another color (e.g., buying one Red unit in a mostly Green economy).
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- miuna6933 (2018-05)

### Sets are classified as 'tactical' when they feature aggressive attackers and low efficient defense, putting emphasis on exact health exchanges and narrow decision windows.
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- namington (2018-06)

### 'Timewalking' refers to invalidating your opponent's entire turn or action for minimal cost, essentially giving yourself an extra turn. Borrowed from Magic: The Gathering terminology.
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- swapgo, cogito3 (2018-07-07)

### The term 'permanent attack' has fallen out of favor in Prismata strategy discussion because it is not a particularly useful metric for comparing positions.
**Confidence:** medium | **Temporal:** historical
> Source: Discord #unknown -- apooche (2018-03)

### Shock: a unit property describing units that threaten damage exceeding their tech cost. Drake threatens 4 damage with 2 tech (positive shock); Valkyrie threatens 4 damage with 4 tech (no shock).
**Units:** Drake, Valkyrie
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- mrguy888 (2018-04)

### 'Big red' refers to any strategy involving a 3+ red cost unit such as Amporilla or Lucina.
**Units:** Amporilla, Lucina
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- wiwiweb (2018-05)

### 'Big clock' units have 4+ :Time: in their cost; 'big blue' units have 3 blue; 'big red' units have 3+ red. These categories describe units with heavy color requirements that serve as economic sinks or late-game threats.
**Units:** Odin, Savior
**Confidence:** high | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- masn6811 (2018-05)

### "Value Wincer" refers to purchasing Wincer for its defensive value (15 HP worth more than 5 drones over 2.5 turns) without firing it, relying on tempo advantage. "Value Wincer Click" (or "Firing Wincer") refers to actually threatening to fire Wincer by then, gaining both the defensive tempo and burst damage.
**Units:** Wincer
**Confidence:** medium | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- masn6811, Deleted User (2018-03)

### "Fastimus" refers to rushing Animus for economy acceleration, often in aggressive or absorb-denial focused sets, as opposed to "natural Animus" which is the default mid-game buy.
**Units:** Animus
**Confidence:** medium | **Temporal:** timeless
> Source: Discord #unknown -- namington, cogito3 (2018-07)
