The goal is to give a rough mathematical value to different units or tradeoffs.
5
Many things are ignored or approximated, like the value of granularity, threat, and flexibility. In actual games, the specific situation is usually more important than theoretical efficiency.
6
Think of these like the heuristic values of chess pieces (Pawn=1, Bishop=3, ...) that are a rough guideline, but with some attempt at quantitative justication.

All "Cost" and "Benefit" values are in gold, as of the turn you're building the unit.
12
 "Diff" is benefit minus cost, so positive is good.
13
 "Eff" is efficiency, which is Diff divided by cost. So, a unit with +10% efficiency gives you 10% more than you paid for.
