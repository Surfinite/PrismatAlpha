==Strategy==
'''{{PAGENAME}}''' is [[tech]] efficient; it provides as much {{ticon|block}}[[defense]] as 2 [[Wall]]s for only one {{ticon|blue}}[[blue]]. Its {{ticon|frontline}}[[Frontline]] mechanism, however, is the drawback to this efficiency.

In the early game, {{PAGENAME}} can absorb all the incoming damage as long as your opponent doesn't have {{ticon|attack||6}} [[attack]]. When this point is reached though, your opponent will be able to destroy it by simply {{ticon|click}}clicking it because of its {{ticon|frontline}}Frontline, denying your [[absorb]].

In the late game, {{PAGENAME}}s can be used to defend efficiently if your opponent is attacking for an amount just shy of a multiple of 6; attacks for {{ticon|attack||11}}, {{ticon|attack||17}}, etc. allow you to absorb {{ticon|attack||5}} damage on your last Polywall. If your opponent's attack is equal to or only one greater than a multiple of six, he will {{ticon|click}}click your {{PAGENAME}}s to destroy them, leaving no or close to no damage to absorb for your final {{ticon|block}}blocker.

{{PAGENAME}} can also be built to save a better absorber by dying in defense, just like you would use standard Walls.

Polywall has the curious effect of actually making multiple [[Blastforge]]s worse in the set despite being a blue unit. This is because it takes {{ticon|gold|10}} to efficiently spend one {{ticon|blue}} on defense in a Polylwall set, so if you have 2 or more Blastforges, you won't be able to spend both of the {{ticon|blue}}blues on Poylwalls unless you have 20 or more [[Drone]]s. Even if you do, this leaves you with no gold to spend the rest of your tech.

Beware of units that provide [[Offensive finesse]] (e.g. [[Pixie]], [[Auride Core]]), as these allow your opponent to attack for exactly 6 or 12 {{ticon|attack}}[[attack|damage]], eliminating Polywall's absorb.
