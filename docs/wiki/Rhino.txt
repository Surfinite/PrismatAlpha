==Strategy==
'''{{PAGENAME}}''' is the red resource basic [[blocker]] unit. It features a somewhat poor amount of {{ticon|block}}[[defense|blocking]] given its cost of {{ticon|gold|5}} [[gold]] and a {{ticon|red}} [[red]] for {{ticon|health|2}} [[Health]]. In terms of raw blocking power, two [[Engineer]]s provide {{ticon|health|2}} of defense for {{ticon|gold|4}} given enough foresight. However, Rhino can [[absorb]] 1 point of {{ticon|attack}} [[attack|damage]], and has {{ticon|prompt}}[[prompt]], allowing a player to use their {{ticon|red}} for immediate blocking purposes. 

{{PAGENAME}} is unique among the basic defenders in that it also has some limited firepower. Since it has {{ticon|stamina|2}} [[Stamina]], it can only attack twice before it is depleted (at which point you can safely discard it on defense).

{{PAGENAME}} is a surprisingly efficient unit for Base set if you are able to get full value from it (2 {{ticon|attack}} then {{ticon|health|2}}). Therefore, you should try to set up a ''{{PAGENAME}} train'', in which you buy {{PAGENAME}}s continuously over multiple turns, attacking twice before defending with the used up body. 

If you are not using any larger absorbers, you can also absorb for one on the {{PAGENAME}} the turn you buy it, since it is prompt. This effect, when used for such train, is like setting {{Ticon|gold|2}} a turn for having a {{ticon|health|2}} absorber, this compared to Advanced set unit power. 

If you manage to keep attacking with the older {{PAGENAME}}s without losing the new ones (for instance covering for them with [[Forcefield]]s), after 3 turns you get in a situation where the older {{PAGENAME}}s 'cover' the new ones until they are done attacking, giving you maximum value on all of them. Knowing the right time to start this train can be tricky. Too early in the game, and it would be better to get a long term attacker, such as a [[Tarsier]], but if you wait too long you will need to sacrifice the {{PAGENAME}}s on defense before they can attack.

{{PAGENAME}}s can also be good value against threatened damage that your opponent doesn't actually want to use. For example, [[Drake]] threatens 2 extra damage by sacrificing a [[Blastforge]], which isn't a great value for the [[Blastforge]]. If you defend the two extra points with a {{PAGENAME}} and your opponent clicks the Drake, then you caused them to use an ability that wasn't actually good value, and if they don't click the Drake, then your Rhino just attacks the next turn and you got away with delaying the need to buy 2 real defense for one turn (and can probably delay it again in the same manner).

Note that the estimated attack value shown during your turn also includes the {{PAGENAME}}s that are currently blocking. [[correct defense|If your opponent has to sacrifice Rhinos in defense, they won't be attacking]]. The same advice also applies to other blockers that can be used to attack, such as [[Steelsplitter]]s.

When you have a {{ticon|stamina|1}} stamina Rhino and a {{ticon|stamina|2}} stamina one and can only afford to attack with one of them, knowing which one to attack with can be tricky. If you attack with the {{ticon|stamina|1}} stamina Rhino, your threatened damage goes down by one sooner, but in return you will have a stamina-less Rhino available to block sooner. Thus attacking with the {{ticon|stamina|1}} stamina Rhino is the more defensive play; attacking with the {{ticon|stamina|2}} stamina Rhino is the more aggressive play.
