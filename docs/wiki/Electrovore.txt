= Electrovore =

{{Unit infobox}}

{{PAGENAME}} is an {{ticon|attack}}[[attack|offensive]] [[{{#var:type}} unit|{{#var:type}}]] [[{{#ifeq:{{lc:{{#var:spell}} }}|yes|spell|unit}}]] that requires {{ticon|energy}}[[Energy]] to attack.

==Panel==
{{Unit panel}}

==Strategy==
'''{{PAGENAME}}''' provides {{ticon|attack}}1 [[attack]] in exchange for {{ticon|energy}}1 [[Energy]]. It costs as much as [[Tarsier]], but only takes 1 turn to construct, allowing you to put pressure earlier. You can also use {{PAGENAME}} to {{ticon|block}}[[blocker|block]], at which it is as efficient as a [[Rhino]], providing either {{ticon|health|2}} [[Health]] of [[defense]] or {{ticon|health}}1 of [[absorb]].

The {{ticon|energy}} consumption of {{PAGENAME}} needs to be accounted for in your build, for instance in the early game if you want to go on increasing your [[Drone]] count or later when [[Engineer]]s start dying in defense every turn. Having to keep Engineers alive (or buying more continuously) is something of a hidden additional cost to the unit.

{{PAGENAME}} comboes nicely with units like [[Antima Comet]], [[Hellhound]] or [[Sentinel]] that end up putting lots of Engineers on your board without using the {{ticon|energy}} themselves.

You generally should buy at least 1-2 Electrovores in games where you wanted an [[Animus]] already. Although you will have to keep buying Engineers to keep them firing, this is okay because in most games you want to be buying some Engineers anyway for [[defensive granularity]], so the first 1-2 Electrovores don't really have that extra cost.

Electrovore can enable powerful rushes if there are no strong defenders in the [[random set]]. Here are example builds for both players. For Player 1:
# DD
# DD
# DDA
# TD + {{PAGENAME}}

For Player 2:
# DD
# DA
# TD + {{PAGENAME}}

Both of those build force your opponent to buy a [[blocker]] one turn earlier than they would if you had gone for Tarsiers.

===Player 2 Rush===
The player 2 {{PAGENAME}} [[rush]] is very powerful, and may not be beatable with just the [[base set]]. The basic premise is that you are using all resources very efficiently (including the {{ticon|energy}}[[Energy]] produced by the first 2 [[Engineer]]s), and using [[Rhino]]s will full efficiency. [[constant attack|Damage]] ramps very quickly until the opponent counterattacks.

====Counters====
To counter the rush, player 1 must find a way to get 7 sustained [[attack]]. If this doesn't seem viable with your random set, player 1 could try a turn 2 {{PAGENAME}} rush. However, this rush is weaker than the player 2 rush, and cannot beat a properly played [[Blastforge]] into [[Conduit]] counterplay unless there are some other {{ticon|red}}red rush support units ([[Immolite]] could help player 1 for example).

Note that the [[Animus]] counter (mirror) is slightly behind in [[tempo]], and loses for player 1 (with proper play from both sides). The Blastforge counter is slow, and allows the player 2 to ramp {{ticon|attack}} [[attack]] considerably. It's only viable if it allows player 1 get a cheap [[pressure]] unit (like [[Tesla Coil]] or [[Plasmafier]]) or a cheap unit with big {{ticon|health}}[[absorb]] (like [[Doomed Wall]] or [[Energy Matrix]]). With only the base set, player 1 needs to spend all resources on defense before he can get 7 {{ticon|attack}}attack.

====Main Lines====
The main [[Blastforge]] line is:
# DD, DD
# DD, DA
# DDB, D1T
# W + Anything, D1T

And the [[Animus]] line is:
# DD, DD
# DD, DA,
# DDA, D11

Until your opponent puts out his first {{ticon|attack}}[[attack]], buy only {{PAGENAME}}s, [[Tarsier]]s, and [[Engineer]]s. Of course, buy {{PAGENAME}}s when you'll have the Engineers to power them next turn, and Tarsiers when you won't. Spend all of your {{ticon|gold}}[[gold]] each turn, as is possible.

After your opponent has {{ticon|attack}}attack, build 1 [[Rhino]] per turn. Calculate your opponent's [[correct defense|max damage]], and determine how many Engineers you'll have left. Buy enough so you can fire all {{PAGENAME}}s next turn. Spend the rest of your money on {{PAGENAME}}s, Tarsiers, and Engineers, as applicable. Ramp {{ticon|attack}}attack as much as possible.

If your opponent [[burst]]s {{ticon|attack}}attack, and you don't have enough {{ticon|block}}[[blocker|defense]], it's better to hold back an {{PAGENAME}} than to hold back a Rhino. You can think of the {{PAGENAME}} as a cheaper Rhino which has paid itself off. Of course, use this tactic situationally. Note that it's almost always wrong to build 2 Rhinos in 1 turn.
