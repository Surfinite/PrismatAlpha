==Strategy==

Innervi Field is medium-efficiency as a defender, having a similar cost to [[Wall]] and the same {{ticon|hp}}. While it has the downside of being [[fragile]], it has several advantages:

* Since it can regenerate {{ticon|hp|1}} per turn, it provides low [[absorb]] to a mono-[[green]] build. This absorb also has the advantage that it cannot be denied by simply dealing 0 damage into Innervi Field. Overall it can be thought of as a sort of mini-[[Xaetron]].
* Since Innervi Field gains health if it doesn't die in defense, it can be used as a counter to [[threat]] units such as [[Frostbite]]: you buy an Innervi Field, and if they don't use the Frostbite, Innervi gains health, rewarding you with an additional 1{{ticon|hp}} in return for building it instead of a Wall. If they do use the Frostbite on a Wall to force you to sacrifice the Innervi Field, then the Frostbite dies, taking the pressure off of your defense.
* Additionally, you can simply buy it one turn early and it functions like an [[Infusion Grid]] - lacking [[Prompt]], but having 4{{ticon|hp}}.

However, beware of constant [[chill]] units - units that can chill repeatedly without paying a cost each time. Against [[Tatsu Nullifier]]s for example, your Innervi Fields will just be frozen repeatedly until they die due to [[Lifespan]], and they will never get to provide actual defensive value.
