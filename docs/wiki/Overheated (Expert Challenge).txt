= Overheated (Expert Challenge) =

__NOTOC__ <!--suppresses table of contents for the headers!-->

Overheated is the third mission of the [[Episode 1|first episode]] in Prismata. Complete all the missions of the [[List of episode 1 missions|episode]] to unlock the expert mission challenges. This mission consists of three basic defensive survival puzzles, where you have to defend only until the Overheat Ray takes out your opponent's units, after which a new challenge starts. Below are hints and the solution for each. <br/><br/>

{|   class="wikitable mw-collapsible mw-collapsed"
| <span style="color:#9EC6F5">'''+ Show general tips&nbsp;'''</span>
|-
| The best ''immediate'' absorb is not always the best (see [[Defense_concepts#Defensive_granularity|Defensive strategies]]) when a few turns are considered. Generally, it is important to figure out which optimal outcome you will have in the next turn too: In the shoes of your opponent, what is the defence that would be terrible to go against? In this mission you can sacrifice drones to that end.  
|-
|Defending with a wall is more cost-efficient than using engineers (5 gold for 3 +absorb instead of 6 gold for 3 [[Health|health]]). Every now and again engineers might be a better buy to keep absorbing damage in upcoming turns and spend all the money available: Set yourself up with an odd numbered gold income for alternating amounts of buyable engineers for turns with an even numbered gold bank.
|-
| The puzzles are set up in ways that often make the unintuitive play the correct one.
|}

=='''Challenge 3'''==

Not having [[Blastforge|Blastforges]] available and not having to live/fight after the Ray shoots simplifies how to assess efficiency and calculate income and costs. Here you can build both Drones and Engineers. They are equally efficient as defenders as soon as the Drone gets clicked once before blocking (net cost of 2; note the difference between ''start of turn'' and ''click''). So you need a drone alive for more than 1 turn (that's more than 2 from the point you paid for it) to more than break-even.
