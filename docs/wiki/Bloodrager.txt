==Strategy==
{{PAGENAME}} provides {{ticon|attack}}[[constant attack]] at the expense of {{ticon|attack}}[[attack|damage]] at construction. As such on its own it is more of an [[econ]] unit than a [[tempo]] unit, since you lose out on an immediate advantage (of dealing {{ticon|attack}}damage) in exchange for increased damage output in the future.

If possible, you want to sneak {{PAGENAME}} in a situation where the {{ticon|attack}}attack it costs would have been [[absorb]]ed anyway to negate that part of its price completely; that this is possible makes {{PAGENAME}} a counter to a very wide range of units who either [[huge blocker counters|give a lot of absorb]] or [[lifespan blocker counters|have both Lifespan and Block]]. Remember that a {{PAGENAME}} which costs only 1 or 0 real attack is actually ''unbelievably'' efficient (even more so than [[Shadowfang]]), thus it is often worth making significant sacrifices to get them out. The cost of a Bloodrager that does not deny Absorb and enemy defense doesn't play, is roughly as efficient as Tarsiers, since an Attack which is delayed is a loss of 2{{Ticon|gold}}, less with efficient Attack and enemy with good Soak/bad Attack.

Beware of buying {{PAGENAME}} in cases where doing {{ticon|attack}}damage immediately is essential (i.e. [[low economy]] {{ticon|red}}[[red]] mirror matches). When your opponent has little absorb, every point of {{ticon|attack}}attack matters; {{PAGENAME}} might just not be fast enough to pay back for itself before you get [[breach]]ed.

==Fast Bloodrager builds==
Since it is so cost-efficient, it is usually tempting to try to rush the Bloodrager early. The setup required is more complicated than for most units however, since to be efficient you want to use all your {{ticon|attack}} on turns you are buying them. Here is an example build for both players:

Player 1:

# DD
# DD
# DDA
# TTD
# TR + ...
# Bloodrager + ... // Just enough attack
# Bloodrager + Bloodrager + ... // Again, all the Attack
It is difficult to get more than one instance though, since both sides may have rising Attack to address from this strategy.

Player 2:

# DD
# DA
# TTD
# TR + ...
# Bloodrager + ...
# Bloodrager + Bloodrager + ... // Will require saving some gold on previous turn

Those builds work exceptionally well against opponents going high econ and just getting defense without putting pressure back, since all the defense they will be buying will be useless before the 3rd Bloodrager hits the board.
