==Strategy==

{{PAGENAME}} is an extremely strong economic unit which can almost never be ignored in a set. Because of its click ability, it is viable to get even in sets without {{ticon|green}} units, since the excess {{ticon|green}} can at least be converted to {{ticon|gold}} (albeit inefficiently).
{{PAGENAME}} is also a strong enabler of [[Defense_strategies#Breachproof|breachproof]] strategies, since it is not difficult to convert almost all of your [[Drone|Drones]] into {{PAGENAME}}s.

If you are buying {{PAGENAME}}s for value but are not going breachproof, you will want 1 to 3 Dynamos depending on how much economy you want before you start full attack production. Even with the faster economy start that {{PAGENAME}} provides, with good defense in the set to discourage breachproof you don't always want to wait for 20 income before the switch. The sweet spot if you don't want many Green units is 3 Dynamos, giving you the option to click a {{PAGENAME}} every turn without floating.

If you are going breachproof, you want to get 4 Dynamos if possible, because the best defense for your {{PAGENAME}} is having more of them. Against aggressive openings, however, it is often better to only make 2-3 Dynamos because your Drones and Engineers can't be defended long enough to assemble the last one and you also need to put the pressure on earlier, so you should avoid overdroning.

==Advanced Strategy==

Compared to [[Trinity Drone]], {{PAGENAME}} generally provides better value, but is less breachproof (and therefore a higher priority target to kill).

If you are breaching an opponent, it will almost always be better to start damaging the {{PAGENAME}}s as opposed to attackers. This is because with the {{PAGENAME}}s alive, you can usually create breachproof attackers faster than they can be destroyed. This may however be less true if there are not many breachproof attackers in the set, or if supplies are running low.

Knowing this, an option for the breachproof player is to let their first {{PAGENAME}} get damaged, but suddenly switch gear to a non-breachproof strategy by starting to [[absorb]]. This "fake breachproof" strategy lets you absorb damage on a {{PAGENAME}} that will keep value the whole game with without actually being finished off. This is very telegraphed by the buying of the [[Blastforge]]. If the opponent can see it coming, they can instead get value by killing off something else (like a [[Gauss Cannon]]) or ensure the destruction of the {{PAGENAME}} by creating too much pressure for 1 turn of defense via threat or chill.

With {{PAGENAME}} in play, Conduits can be bought for a similar value as a Drone, as they provide one {{ticon|green}} which can be turned to {{ticon|gold}}. However, this is very inefficient as you would basically never want to buy a Drone for {{ticon|gold|4}}.

==Efficiency==

You can think about {{PAGENAME}} by comparing it to purchasing an equivalent amount of [[Drone|Drones]] and [[Conduit|Conduits]]. Suppose you have six [[Drone|Drones]] and two [[Engineer|Engineers]]. The following sequences result in identical economic outcomes:

===== Line A (Dynamo) =====
# Dynamo (bank 1)
# (bank 4)

===== Line B (DD+C) =====
# DD (bank 0)
# C (bank 4)

However, the {{PAGENAME}} is buyable even if you only have 5{{ticon|gold}}, or only one {{ticon|energy}}, whereas you needed {{ticon|energy|2}} to play DD/C. This may free up {{ticon|energy}} to produce more [[Drone|Drones]] simultaneously. This provides a significant economic advantage in scenarios where you would otherwise have had to e.g. buy a single [[Drone]] and bank 2{{ticon|gold}}.
