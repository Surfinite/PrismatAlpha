= Centrifuge =

==Strategy==
'''{{PAGENAME}}''' provides you with a huge [[economic tempo|burst]] of [[resource]]s (including all 3 colored ones) {{ticon|build|3}} turns after you buy it (giving back  {{ticon|gold}}[[gold]]). As it gives you for a single turn access to [[technology|technologies]] that would otherwise require a heavy investment in tech buildings, it is most useful when it lets you buy [[supply|legendary]] units like [[Odin]] or [[Lucina Spinos]]; the main point of the unit is to use the {{ticon|blue}}[[blue]] and {{ticon|red}}[[red]] immediately as they are discarded otherwise.

Keep in mind however that {{PAGENAME}}'s {{ticon|gold}}cost will often go to build it in the early game. This will put you one turn behind an opponent forgoing {{PAGENAME}}. The payoff has to be impressive to compensate for the disadvantage.

As such, if the [[random set]] doesn't have relevant legendary units, going for {{PAGENAME}} is generally a bad play, as you would have been better off simply [[econ|econing up]] into an economy letting you produce constantly the {{ticon|attack}}[[attack]]ers and {{ticon|block}}[[blocker]]s you need. You can still consider it to get a one-off of an [[absorb]]er such as [[Energy Matrix]], but it's not a strong enough play to mandate cutting your economy early for it. On the same note, if the legendary you plan to get is a [[defense|defensive]] one ([[Centurion]], [[Defense Grid]]), it is best to plan your {{PAGENAME}} to make use of their high absorb as soon as they come into play. Going {{PAGENAME}} doesn't change the fact that you should go for [[offense over defense]] when you can.
